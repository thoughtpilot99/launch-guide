# Template — Agency Amplification Post

**Use for:** Agencies, ghostwriters, or production partners posting about a client launch they shipped. Different voice from the client's own post. Goal: social proof for the agency, plus implicit prospecting.

**Length:** Single tweet OR 2-3 tweet mini-thread. Rarely longer.

**Real anchors:** Represent's client amplifications, Shown Media / Matt Epstein nuclear-launch posts, Social Capital Inc's Icon case-study posts.

---

## The rule

**Result-first. The result IS the pitch.**

Don't lead with "we worked with [client]." Lead with what the client did. The agency name in the byline does the rest of the work.

---

## Fill-in skeleton — single tweet

```
[CLIENT] did [ABSURD METRIC]. Here's the [60-second video / post / launch asset].

[1-2 line teaser of what's remarkable]

[VIDEO EMBEDDED]
```

**Reply tweet (optional soft pitch):**
```
We take 3 launch slots per quarter. If your launch is coming up, DM.
```

---

## Fill-in skeleton — mini-thread (2-3 tweets)

```
Tweet 1 (RESULT-FIRST HOOK):
[Client] did [absurd metric]. Here's the launch.

[VIDEO EMBEDDED]

Tweet 2 (PROCESS/CREDIT):
[3-week turnaround. Script + storyboard + live-action + motion.]
[What was hard. What unlocked it.]

Tweet 3 (SECOND-ORDER / SOFT CTA):
[Week 1: [first-order metric]. Month 3: [second-order metric like inbound/pipeline/hiring].]
[We take 3 launch slots per quarter. If yours is coming up, DM.]
```

## The "second-order" variant (for post-launch retrospectives)

**Use when:** 1-6 months after a launch. The first-order numbers are in. The second-order effects (inbound / hiring / press / partnerships) have compounded.

```
Tweet 1:
[Client] launched [N] months ago.

Here's what the video is still doing today:

Tweet 2:
Week 1: [first-order metric, big number]
Month 1: [continued momentum]
Month 3: [second-order effect — inbound pipeline, partnerships]
Month 6: [third-order — hiring, brand memory, acquirer attention]

Tweet 3:
This is why we tell every founder: the launch video isn't the launch. It's the first frame of a 12-month asset.

We take [N] launch slots per quarter.
```

## Pre-ship checklist

- [ ] First line: CLIENT'S RESULT, not "we worked with"
- [ ] Specific metric in first 10 words
- [ ] Video embedded at tweet 1 (or mini-thread tweet 1)
- [ ] Process description is 1-2 lines max (reader cares about the result, not the production)
- [ ] Named team credit (your team + client's key person)
- [ ] Soft service plug at the end or in reply (never in tweet 1)
- [ ] No em dashes
- [ ] Tag client with permission ONLY
- [ ] Don't gloat. Celebrate the client.

---

## Anti-patterns

❌ "We're thrilled to have worked with [client]" — throat-clear, dead on arrival
❌ "Our team did [process description]" — nobody cares about your process before they care about the result
❌ Multiple client logos in one post — reads as portfolio-dumping
❌ Stacking the hard sell ("We do video + post + influencer amp + analytics, DM now") — one pitch at most, as a soft ask
❌ Tagging a client without permission — relationship-ending move
❌ Taking credit for the video IF the client wrote the post — separate out "we shipped the video" from "they wrote the post" clearly

---

## The compounding benefit

Agency amplification posts accomplish THREE things in one:

1. **Social proof** for the agency
2. **Case study** for the next prospect
3. **Favor** for the client (you're promoting their launch from your audience)

Do this right and the client QRTs you back. Their audience + your audience = 2-3x the reach of either alone. Plus the favor deepens the relationship for future work.
