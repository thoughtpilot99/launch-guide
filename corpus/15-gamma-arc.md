## Post 4 — Founder Story (Jan 2026, LinkedIn)

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/grantslee_gamma-didnt-start-with-ai-it-started-with-activity-7410349072529948672-X8w-

> Gamma didn't start with AI. It started with three co-founders, five years at a company that didn't work out like we had hoped, and a chip on our shoulder we couldn't shake. Everything about our pitch screamed "bad idea." But that was weirdly motivating:
>
> 2020: Peak pandemic. I was living in London, whisper-pitching U.S. investors from 8pm to 2am in the tiny space between our kitchenette and the laundry room. In my third pitch of the first day, an investor stared at me and said: "This has to be the worst idea I've ever heard." Massive incumbents. Deep distribution. 40 years of feature development. Then he just hung up.
>
> A first-time founder might pivot after that. A second-time founder with a chip writes it down and makes it part of the spec. He was right about the difficulty. So we treated growth and distribution as core product problems, not marketing problems to outsource later.
>
> 2022: Launched on Product Hunt. Won product of the day, week, and month. Got 60,000 signups. Then growth flattened. On paper it looked good. In our guts, it felt wrong. So we didn't declare victory and buy ads. We paused and rebuilt. Three months. Twelve people. One goal: make the first 30 seconds feel magical. We wired AI directly into onboarding. Your first experience was going from a vague idea to a fully-formed, decent-looking deck with one prompt. The blank page problem evaporated.
>
> 2023: Signups went from hundreds a day to tens of thousands. No paid marketing.
>
> 2025: $100M ARR. 70 million users. Profitable for two years. Around 50 people.
>
> That's five years of not letting go. Three people who believed that there had to be a better way. Five years at a previous company that didn't end how we wanted. A decision not to let any of it go to waste.
>
> If you've failed before, don't try to get rid of the chip on your shoulder. Aim it in the right direction.

**Pattern:** **Year-stamped chapters** (2020 → 2022 → 2023 → 2025). Each year = one beat. Clean pacing.

**Signature tic:** Hostile-investor quote as scene-setter ("This has to be the worst idea I've ever heard") + **"Then he just hung up."** — that's the sentence that makes the post memorable.

**Close:** Actionable advice ("don't try to get rid of the chip... aim it in the right direction") — turns his story into reader's story.

**Lessons for the skill:**
- Year-stamped chapters = cleanest pacing for founder-origin posts. 2020 → 2022 → 2023 → 2025.
- A named antagonist (investor who hung up) carries more weight than 5 paragraphs of abstract difficulty.
- Close with reader-facing advice that makes your story their story.
