# Icon — The Rosetta Stone Launch Post

Kennan Davison runs Icon AND co-founded Social Capital Inc (sociallcapital.com). Icon's launch IS the template Social Capital Inc pitches to clients. If you reverse-engineer one launch end-to-end, this is it.

## Formula (from public breakdown, ordered)

1. **Category claim:** "Introducing Icon, the world's first AI Admaker"
2. **Analogy bridge:** "ChatGPT + CapCut, but for making winning ads with AI in minutes"
3. **Investor name-drops:** Founders Fund / Peter Thiel
4. **Quantified value:** "teams making 30 ads/month → 300/month"
5. **Social-proof brand list:** "Ridge, Jones Road, Immi, Backbone, MUD\WTR — all $100M+ revenue brands"
6. **Price wedge:** "$399/month vs competitors at $2K-$30K/month"
7. **Lead magnet INSIDE the launch post:** "free Google Drive with 1000 winning ads from brands doing $20M+ in revenue"

## Why this is the gold standard

**The structure compresses 6 distinct persuasion moves into one post:**
- Category claim = positioning
- Analogy = comprehension
- Investors = credibility
- Quantified value = ROI math
- Social proof = trust
- Price wedge = conversion trigger
- Lead magnet = distribution loop

**The lead magnet INSIDE the launch post is the underrated move.** Everyone gates "swipe files" behind email forms. Icon put it in a public Google Drive. Every person who downloaded it then shared it, and the post compounded. This is Social Capital Inc's signature trick.

## Lessons for the skill

1. **The 7-beat template (category → analogy → investor → value → social proof → price → lead magnet) is the most reusable launch structure extracted from the corpus.** More durable than origin-story threads, more repeatable than viral stunts.
2. **Price wedges work when the delta is ≥5x.** "$399 vs $2K-$30K" is believable; "$99 vs $129" is not.
3. **Public lead magnets beat gated ones for distribution.** Gate only when you need to capture email. For launch-day virality, leave the asset open.
4. **Analogy bridges are high-leverage.** "ChatGPT + CapCut" gets Icon past explanation in 4 words. Most launch posts waste 2 paragraphs doing what 4 words of analogy would've done.
