# The YC Launch HN Classics

These are the 7 highest-leverage YC-era launches. The HN title alone did most of the work in each case.

---

## Supabase — "An open source Firebase alternative"

**Founder:** Paul Copplestone (@kiwicopple)
**HN:** https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=23319901 (May 27, 2020)
**Engagement:** 1,120 points / 366 comments — second most-upvoted Launch HN of all time.

**Verbatim title:**
> Supabase (YC S20) – An open source Firebase alternative

**Why it works:** The title IS the positioning. "Open source [category leader] alternative" — 6 words do all the work. Body was secondary.

**Impact:** Jumped Supabase from 80 → 800 users overnight via the HN front page.

---

## PostHog — "Open-source product analytics"

**Founders:** James Hawkins (@james406), Tim Glaser, Aaron
**HN:** https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=22376732 (Feb 20, 2020)

**Verbatim opening:**
> James, Tim and Aaron here - we are building a self-hosted, open source Mixpanel/Amplitude style product.

**Companion X post:**
> We hated sending user data to 3rd party analytics. I'm delighted to announce that our open source product analytics are now live. Self-hosted, full underlying SQL/API data access. 1 click Heroku deploy

**Why it works:** "We hated X" = resentment hook. First-person plural founder intro = YC canon. The tech-stack line (Heroku one-click) short-circuits "but can I try it?"

## Resend — "Email API for developers using React"

**Founder:** Zeno Rocha (@zenorocha)
**HN:** https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=36309120 (June 13, 2023)
**X engagement:** Announcement tweet exceeded 1M views.

**Verbatim opening:**
> Hi HN! I'm Zeno, founder of Resend (https://resend.com). We're building a modern email sending platform focused on providing the best developer experience.

**Verbatim closing line:**
> We need to stop developing emails like it's 2010 and rethink how email can be done in 2023 and beyond.

**Two-year-anniversary X post:**
> Two years ago, Resend was the new kid on the block. Many people told me, 'We don't need another Email API.' But if you focus on quality, there's always space, even in a super crowded market. We still have a looong way to go, and there's so much more to build, so back to work.

**Why it works:** Names competitors by name (SendGrid, Mailgun, Postmark, SparkPost), acknowledges they were founded 2009-2010, flips the "crowded market" objection, personal "I" voice. Closer is a time-anchor: "stop developing emails like it's 2010."

## Replicate — "Version control for machine learning"

**Founders:** Ben Firshman (@bfirsh), Andreas Jansson
**HN:** https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=25150638 (Nov 19, 2020)

**Verbatim opening:**
> We're Ben and Andreas. We're building a lightweight open-source tool for tracking and analyzing your machine learning experiments.

**Verbatim core wedge:**
> Git doesn't work well with machine learning. It can't store trained machine learning models, it can't handle key/value metadata, and it's not designed to record information automatically from a training script.

**Why it works:** "[Existing thing] doesn't work well with [new domain]" — one of the cleanest wedge sentences in the corpus. "Small, easy to use, extensible" philosophy.

## Cal.com (Calendso) — "Open source Calendly alternative"

**Founder:** Bailey Pumfleet (@baileypumfleet)
**HN:** https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=26817795 (April 15, 2021)
**Engagement:** 311 points, 91 comments + #1 Product Hunt of the day, week, and month (April 2021).

**Verbatim title:**
> Calendso: An open source Calendly alternative

**Why it works:** Pure title-as-positioning. Same template as Supabase. Body was lighter than Supabase's; the positioning + repo did the work.

## Raycast — "CLI-inspired desktop app"

**Founders:** Thomas Paul Mann, Petr Nikolaev
**HN:** https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=22466994 (March 2, 2020)

**Verbatim opening:**
> Thomas and Petr here. Raycast is a command-line inspired native app to save developers time on non-coding tasks, such as managing active sprints in Jira, sorting out notifications in GitHub, or checking metrics in Amplitude.

**Verbatim problem framing:**
> To update the status of an issue, you have to open the browser, create a new tab, navigate to the website and find the right issue.

**Why it works:** Mundane concrete example makes the pain visible. Three named integrations (Jira, GitHub, Amplitude) as proof of breadth.

**URL:** https://medium.com/linear-app/announcing-linear-e64e79581d01 (April 18, 2019)

## The YC-HN template (condensed for the skill)

**Title:** `[Product] (YC [Batch]) – [Open source / modern / minimalist] [category leader] alternative for [audience]`

**Body:**
1. "Hi HN! I'm [Name], founder of [Product]." OR "We're [Name] and [Name]."
2. One sentence: what it does, in the driest possible terms.
3. Why now / why this wasn't solved (name competitors by name).
4. Wedge: "[Existing thing] doesn't work well with [new domain]." OR "Nobody is building an exceptional [X]."
5. Core features in 3-5 bullets.
6. Tech-stack short-circuit ("1-click Heroku deploy" / "works with any stack").
7. License + monetization model.
8. "Would love your feedback" close.

**When to use:** Dev-tools, infra, open-source anything, technical audience. Do NOT use for consumer apps, B2B SaaS with non-technical buyers, or anything where the category leader is NOT universally known.
