# The AI-Era Demo-First Launches

2024-2026 rewrote launch-post conventions. The demo video is the post. The text is the title card. These 5 launches define the pattern.

---

## Devin — "The first AI software engineer"

**Author:** Scott Wu (@ScottWu46), Cognition Labs
**Date:** March 12, 2024
**Blog:** https://cognition.ai/blog/introducing-devin
**Engagement:** Demo video exceeded 30M views on X by Jan 2026 — among the most-viewed launch demos ever.

**Verbatim blog opening:**
> Today we're excited to introduce Devin, the first AI software engineer.

**Verbatim positioning:**
> Devin is a tireless, skilled teammate, equally ready to build alongside you or independently complete tasks for you to review.

**Verbatim benchmark flex:**
> Devin correctly resolves 13.86%* of the issues end-to-end, far exceeding the previous state-of-the-art of 1.96%.

**Why it works:** "First [category]" positioning + benchmark number (13.86% vs 1.96% = 7x) + autonomous-agent demo. Patrick Collison QRT amplified it massively.

**Pattern:** "Today we're excited to introduce [X], the first [category]." + benchmark vs. SOTA + demo video.

## Bolt.new — "what do you want to build?"

**Author:** Eric Simons (@ericsimons), CEO StackBlitz (@boltdotnew)
**Date:** October 3, 2024
**URL:** https://x.com/boltdotnew/status/1841873251313844631
**Engagement:** Day 1 → $60K ARR. Day 2 → $80K ARR. Week 1 → $1M ARR. Month 1 → $4M ARR.

**Verbatim hook (operative line):**
> what do you want to build?

**Why it works:** Anti-hook hook. Lowercase. 5 words. Doubles as the product UI's empty-state prompt. Demo video is the post; text is a prompt.

**Pattern:** Question as hook. Lowercase. Demo video does all persuasion. **This is the hardest hook to nail — requires a genuinely magical demo.**

**Author:** Vercel (@rauchg as primary amplifier)
**Date:** October 11, 2023
**URL:** https://vercel.com/blog/announcing-v0-generative-ui

**Verbatim opening:**
> A few weeks ago, we introduced v0: a product that makes website creation as simple as describing your ideas. We call it Generative UI — combining the best practices of frontend development with the potential of generative AI.

**Verbatim closing CTA:**

**Why it works:** Coined a category name on launch ("Generative UI") — the headless/serverless move. CTA is a question, mirroring Bolt.

## The AI-era template (distilled)

**Text:**
- 1-3 sentences max
- Often starts with lowercase or a question
- Either coins a category ("Generative UI", "AI software engineer", "General AI agent") OR asks a question ("what do you want to build?")
- No founder backstory in the main tweet (save it for the follow-up)

**Video:**
- 30-90 seconds
- Screen capture with captions burned in
- Shows product solving 3-5 distinct problems in one cut
- No narration usually (music + captions)
- No talking head intro

**CTA:**
- Link in replies, not main tweet
- Waitlist or direct product access, not demo booking
- Question closer ("what will you ship?") is a signature move

**Amplification:**
- Tag one big-audience investor or operator (Patrick Collison, Jack Dorsey, Garry Tan) who might QRT
- The QRT is the accelerant; without it, a great demo still needs distribution

**When NOT to use:**
- Your product demo isn't magical
- Your audience is not AI-Twitter
- You need to explain context before showing
- You're launching a non-AI product (this template is AI-coded now; feels off for non-AI launches)
