# Chasi — "part of Y Combinator's Winter 2026 batch"

## Verbatim post

> Chasi is now part of Y Combinator's Winter 2026 batch! YC has backed companies like Airbnb, DoorDash, EquipmentShare (and a lot of the most important startups of the last 20 years). We're grateful to be part of the community. Over the last few months, Sarman Aulakh and I have traveled thousands of miles across North America, meeting the people who keep the real economy running, teams selling, renting, or leasing everything from mining trucks to forklifts to power tools. Almost all of these businesses run on strong relationships and trust. But both buyers and sellers hate how much time gets burned on admin. No matter if you run a small or large dealer network, rental shop, or parts counter, your day is basically: back-to-back phone calls, emails, follow-ups… booking, scheduling, proposals, contracts, insurance, loans… and when things go wrong, everyone expects their issues to be fixed ASAP. One thing became obvious fast: The real built world doesn't want more software. They hate updating CRMs/ERPs. They don't want more emails, more tabs, more 'log in to see status.' But everyone still wants to grow faster, retain customers, and build a business that scales. Chasi has already helped a handful of equipment companies tackle this by enabling their teams to leverage AI in practical ways. History shows: when you remove friction and improve how goods + information flow (roads, rail, electricity, the internet), people build more, waste less, and create abundance. We believe AI is the next major commerce enabler, and industrial commerce is where it matters most. Grateful to our early customers, investors, advisors, and partners who believed in this vision and supported us. We're just getting started!

1. YC announce
2. Credential stack ("Airbnb, DoorDash, EquipmentShare" — note the vertical tell with EquipmentShare)
3. Field-research narrative ("traveled thousands of miles")
4. ICP empathy (admin burnout list)

**Signature tics:**
- Third name-drop as vertical tell (Airbnb/DoorDash/**EquipmentShare**)
- Ellipses in the pain list ("booking, scheduling, proposals, contracts, insurance, loans…")
- Historical parallel (roads/rail/electricity/internet) — classic founder-thesis move
- "Just getting started" close

**Why it works (459/85 engagement):** The field research beat ("traveled thousands of miles") transforms a YC announcement into a story of commitment. The "real built world doesn't want more software" line is a quotable contrarian.

**Lessons for the skill:**
- Third name-drop as vertical tell — use one expected (Airbnb), one well-known (DoorDash), and one that signals your specific vertical (EquipmentShare for industrial).
- Ellipses in a pain list adds rhythm, gives the feeling of "so much more..."
- Historical parallels (roads/rail/electricity/internet) are force multipliers — use sparingly, one per post.
