## Pattern analysis

**Post hook:** Rocket + "Launching Bilanc (YC W24): AI-Powered Engineering Productivity" — **functional, weak**. Generic announcement. The post is a wrapper; the **video hook** is the real punch.

**Video hook:** "50 commits beats five commits, right?" — **strong**. Question + numerical contrast + setup for reversal. This is the pattern the post should've matched.

**Structural beats (post):**
1. Rocket emoji + "Launching X (YC W24): Y"
2. Excitement statement
3. Scale claim (millions of LoC, hundreds of reports)
4. Social proof (GoFundMe, Runway)
5. Product mechanic
6. Topical hook ("Need to justify AI costs?")
7. Soft CTA + 4 rocket emojis

**Signature tics:** 🚀 bookends, "Bilanc to the world!!!!", question-as-pitch-pivot ("Need to justify AI costs?")

## Anti-pattern lesson

**Bilanc is the cautionary tale.** The video does the launch. The post is corporate boilerplate ("most powerful," "most robust," "many more"). Yet it pulled 100K views and 877 reactions because:
1. The video is excellent
2. Samuel has personal network depth in the YC W24 cohort
3. The audience already knows engineering metrics is a real problem

**If the video weren't this strong, the post would have died.** A good post-writing skill should NEVER produce copy that needs the video to do the work. The post hook should be as strong as the video hook.

**ROI receipt:** Per Renat's case study notes, this single post + video paid for itself in customer revenue ($1-2K MRR per new customer × small handful of converted leads).

## Lessons for the skill

- **Match the video hook in the post.** If the video opens with a question, the post should open with the same question (or a tighter version).
- **Avoid stacking superlatives** ("most powerful," "most robust"). Pick one specific claim.
- **Topical hooks ("Need to justify AI costs?")** add urgency but feel salesy if they aren't the lead.
- A weak post + great video can still hit, but it's leaving 2-3x on the table.
