# Length & Format Decision

A launch post is one of three lengths. The video is constant; the text wrapper around it varies. Pick length **before** drafting.

---

## Three lengths, one decision

### A. Single tweet (≤280 chars)
- 1 tweet, video attached.
- Reads in 5 seconds.
- Highest scroll-recoverability.
- Best when: product name + one-line value-prop is obvious; founder/brand has audience to carry it; the video itself does the persuasion.
- Worst when: product needs explanation, story is the asset, or there's no warm audience.

**Real anchors:** Linear v1 launch, most YC company day-of tweets, Cursor 0.1 launch.

---

### B. Long tweet (X Premium, up to 25,000 chars)
- Single post, no thread.
- Reads as a "mini-essay" inside the timeline. No "1/" "2/" hashes.
- Newer format (rolled out 2023, normalized 2024-2025).
- Best when: the **story** is the hook but you want unified threading metrics + no risk of tweet-2 drop-off; LinkedIn-style narrative on X; want to give context to a video without 12 separate tweets.
- Worst when: hook needs a hard cliffhanger, audience is mostly mobile (long tweets get truncated to "Show more" — same drop-off risk as tweet-2 in a thread).

**Why founders choose this:**
- One unit of engagement (one like, one retweet, one bookmark per reader)
- Algorithm rewards dwell-time, and long tweets get high dwell
- No "thread fatigue" reaction
- Easier to read on desktop than scroll through 12 tweets

**Real anchors:** Many AI-era launches (Devin, Cognition Labs), some PJ Ace AI-ad reveals, Pieter Levels milestones.

---

### C. Numbered thread (5-15 tweets)
- 1/N format OR no numbers, just connected replies.
- Each tweet 240-280 chars.
- Reads as a paced narrative.
- Best when: story has clear beats (problem → discovery → build → reveal → results); product is technical and benefits from progressive disclosure; founder wants multiple engagement units per reader (each tweet can be liked/RT'd separately).
- Worst when: story is thin (forced beats are visible), audience attention is shallow, or you don't have 5 strong things to say.

**Real anchors:** Devin Cognition Labs launch thread, Lovable rebrand thread, every "I started a company" origin thread.

## Decision matrix

| Variable | Single tweet | Long tweet | Thread |
|---|---|---|---|
| Audience knows you | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Audience is cold | ⚠️ relies on video | ✅ context helps | ✅ story helps |
| Product is self-explanatory | ✅ | ⚠️ overkill | ❌ pads |
| Product needs setup | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Story is the asset | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Numbers/results are the asset | ✅ | ✅ | ⚠️ depends |
| Video is brand/emotional | ✅ | ✅ | ⚠️ thread dilutes |
| Video is feature-walkthrough | ⚠️ | ✅ | ✅ |
| AI-era "look at this build" reveal | ⚠️ | ✅ best | ⚠️ over-formal |
| Want multiple engagement units | ❌ 1 | ❌ 1 | ✅ N |

---

## Length-by-archetype default

| Archetype | Default length | Stretch target |
|---|---|---|
| 1. YC-style announcement | Single tweet | 1 tweet + 1 reply with link |
| 2. Origin story | Thread (8-12) | Long tweet if story is tight |
| 3. Viral teardown / reveal | Thread (10-15) | Long tweet if reveal is one moment |
| 4. Series A / fundraise | Thread (5-8) | Long tweet (becoming default 2025+) |
| 5. Lead-magnet / free resource | Single tweet | Long tweet only if explaining HOW to use it |
| 6. Agency amplification | Single tweet OR mini-thread (2-3) | Avoid long tweet — sounds boastful |

---

## The "convert between lengths" rule

Same launch can ship in two lengths to two audiences:
- **Day 1:** thread on X (your audience)
- **Day 2:** long tweet on X with a sharper hook (test against the thread)
- **Day 3:** LinkedIn post (different register, same content)
- **Day 4:** single tweet teaser pointing back to the thread

**Don't waste the asset on one format.** The launch video is expensive. The post is cheap. Multiply the post around the video.

---

## Long-tweet specific craft notes

1. **First 210 chars must earn "Show more."** Treat it like an email subject + preview text combo.
2. **No more than 4 paragraph breaks.** Each break is a re-engagement risk.
3. **Embed video in the post, not a reply.** Long tweet + video in same unit performs best.
4. **End with a single, specific CTA.** Don't stack three.
5. **Avoid bullet lists in long tweets.** They feel LinkedIn-y. Use short sentence-paragraphs instead.
6. **Numbers in the first line.** Long tweet without an early number is just a blog post.

---

## Thread-specific craft notes

1. **Tweet 1 must stand alone.** 60% of readers see only it. If they don't unfurl, your thread is wasted.
2. **Tweet 2 has the highest drop-off (~50%).** Put a payoff or tease here, not connective tissue.
3. **Number your tweets (1/, 2/) only if you have <8.** More than that and the numbering feels heavy.
4. **Hide the link in the LAST reply, not the main thread.** The algorithm penalizes links in main posts.
5. **Embed video at tweet 1 OR tweet 5-6 (mid-reveal).** Never at the end — the audience that scrolled won't reward you.
6. **End with a CTA and a "RT the first tweet" ask.** This works on threads, never on single tweets.

---

## Single-tweet specific craft notes

1. **Use line breaks.** A single tweet with 4 line breaks reads "spacious" and gets more dwell.
2. **One specific noun in the first line.** Product name, customer name, dollar figure, time. Not "we" or "I".
3. **Video does the work.** If the tweet says less than the video, the tweet is doing its job.
4. **No more than one URL.** If you need two (signup + waitlist), put one in a reply.
