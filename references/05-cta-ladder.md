# CTA Ladder — From Soft to Hard, When to Use Each

The CTA is the last line of the post (or the link in the reply). It determines conversion path: signup, discovery call, lead magnet, follow, QRT, or brand awareness.

**Rule:** pick ONE CTA. Stacking two dilutes both.

---

## The 7 CTA types, ranked softest to hardest

### 1. Brand Thesis (softest)
**Form:** A declarative sentence that summarizes your positioning. No action requested.

**Examples:**
- "This is the age of the builder." (Anton Osika, $330M)
- "All good things start small and shaky." (Alex Bouaziz, $300M)
- "Aim the chip in the right direction." (Grant Lee, Gamma origin)

**When to use:** Fundraise announcements. Origin stories. When audience is warm and the point is positioning, not conversion.
**Why it works:** Quotable. Gets screenshotted. Reader feels positioned next to your thesis.
**Conversion path:** Reader clicks to bio → reads tagline → clicks product.

---

### 2. Inclusive Invitation
**Form:** "Come [verb] your [N specific example roles]."

**Examples:**
- "Come launch your portfolio, side hustle, project, course, and anything else you've been dreaming of." (Gamma Sites)
- "Come build with us."

**When to use:** Consumer/prosumer products. Welcoming audiences who aren't sure they're the ICP.
**Why it works:** The 5 specific roles let the reader self-identify. Inclusive but not vague.

---

### 3. Direct Founder Contact
**Form:** "DM me" / "email me at founders@..." / "reply if you want X."

**Examples:**
- "Email me: arlan@nozomio.com" (Nia/Nozomio)
- "founders@humwork.ai" (Humwork)
- "send me a note or join the waitlist on our website" (Trava)
- "If you oversee compliance or finance and want to see what Trava can uncover, send me a note"

**When to use:** Enterprise / high-consideration products. Technical B2B. Small list of target customers.
**Why it works:** Low-barrier. Self-selects high-intent readers. Founder in the sales loop signals accessibility.

---

### 4. Segmented Multi-CTA
**Form:** "Three asks: [audience 1] — [action]. [audience 2] — [action]. [audience 3] — [action]."

**Examples:**
- "Three asks: developers building with AI agents — try us. Companies running internal agents — let's talk. Domain experts — join the network and get paid." (Humwork)
- "→ Try it: [link]. → Book a demo: [link]. → Email me: [email]." (Nia)

**When to use:** Marketplaces, multi-sided platforms. When the product serves 2+ distinct audiences.
**Why it works:** Each reader finds their lane. No generic "sign up" that fits nobody specifically.

---

### 5. Comment-Gated Access
**Form:** "Comment '[keyword]' for [thing]."

**Examples:**
- "-> Comment 'Crunched' below, and we'll give you and your firm early access <-" (Crunched)
- "Drop a 👀 below if you want the template" (common LinkedIn pattern)

**When to use:** Launch day only. Drives algorithmic boost on LinkedIn specifically. Captures email via comment → DM → follow-up.
**Why it works:** Comment count inflates reach 3-5x on LinkedIn. Captures intent.
**Trade-off:** Feels "engagement bait" if used weekly. Reserve for launch day, big milestones.

---

### 6. Contest / UGC CTA
**Form:** "Build/share [X] with [product] and tag us. Winner gets [prize]."

**Examples:**
- "build a 2.0 version of any product with lovable and quote this tweet with it our favorite by monday wins $8k" (Lovable 2.0)

**When to use:** Product launches where UGC demonstrates capability. Agencies announcing tool launches.
**Why it works:** Each entry is a distribution unit. Prize = marketing spend disguised as generosity.
**Trade-off:** Works only if the product is quick to demo. If it takes 10 hours to produce an entry, you get 3 entries.

---

### 7. Direct Product Link (hardest, highest-intent)
**Form:** "[URL]" or "Try it: [URL]" or "Sign up at [URL]."

**When to use:** Product is self-explanatory, audience is pre-warmed, no story needed.
**Why it rarely works alone:** Reader hasn't committed to reading. Direct link in main post = scroll-past. Put the link in a reply tweet instead.

**Best practice:** Link in REPLY tweet, not main tweet. The algorithm penalizes external links in main posts on X.

---

## The CTA-by-archetype matrix

| Archetype | Default CTA |
|---|---|
| 1. YC-style single tweet | Direct product link in reply |
| 2. Origin thread | Brand thesis OR founder DM |
| 3. Viral teardown | Brand thesis OR inclusive invitation |
| 4. Fundraise announcement | Brand thesis only |
| 5. Lead-magnet | Comment-gated OR direct link |
| 6. Agency amplification | Direct founder DM ("DM to book discovery") |

## Adding the upsell/service-plug layer

For agency posts where a free resource is the top of funnel but the service is the actual offer:

**Structure:**
1. Lead with the free resource.
2. Show its value with 1-2 sentences.
3. Plug the service in the LAST SENTENCE of the post (not in the middle).

**Example:**
> "Absolutely fucked that this is free. Here's our launch-day checklist — 47 items we ship every launch. [Link]. We take 3 launch slots per quarter. If your launch is coming up, DM."

**Why last-sentence:** The reader who's getting the free thing and also wants to hire you has already committed to reading. A service line in the middle would feel salesy. A service line at the end feels like a soft "by the way."

---

## CTA anti-patterns

❌ **Stacked CTAs:** "Try it + follow us + sign up for the newsletter + join Discord" — picks 4, converts 0.
❌ **Watch the video:** Already embedded. Redundant.
❌ **"Link in bio":** Requires the reader to leave, navigate, find, click. Drop-off cliff.
❌ **"Thoughts?"** at the end of a launch post: asks the reader to do work. Only works in teaching/teardown posts.
❌ **"RT if you agree":** Obvious engagement bait. Lowers algorithm trust.
❌ **"Tell me what you want to see next":** Launches aren't feedback polls.
