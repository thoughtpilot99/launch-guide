# Worked Example — Formula → Real Draft

The best way to understand the playbook is to see the formula applied in real time. Here's a fictional product taken from blank page → ship-ready post in 5 minutes.

---

## The setup

**Product:** Tally — an AI assistant that turns CRM activity logs into weekly performance reports for sales managers.

**Founder:** Maya Chen. Ex-Salesforce PM. First-time founder.

**Stage:** Day-of launch. Has a 60-second product demo video.

**Audience:** ~2K X followers (technical founder audience). Building in public for 6 months.

**Goal:** Drive 100 pilot signups in the first week.

---

## Step 1 — Pick the archetype

**Decision:**
- Has audience (~2K) — single tweet works
- Product is self-explanatory (AI for sales reports) — single tweet works
- Has demo video — single tweet works
- First-time founder — origin angle could help, but small audience = single tweet first, origin thread later

→ **Archetype 1: YC-style single tweet announcement** (with origin thread as Day 2 follow-up)

---

## Step 2 — Pick the length

→ **Single tweet on X** + reply with link.
→ **Long post on LinkedIn** for the LinkedIn audience.

---

## Step 3 — Draft using the 5 beats

### Beat 1 — Clear announcement
First draft: "Today we're launching Tally — AI for sales reports."

Test:
- 8 words, contains product name + category. ✅
- "AI for sales reports" is too generic. Sharpen.

Revised: "Introducing Tally — the AI that writes your weekly sales reports while you sleep."

Better. The "while you sleep" is the differentiator (automation, set-and-forget).

### Beat 2 — Problem statement (twist the knife)
First draft: "Sales reports take a long time."

Weak. Adjective check.

Revised: "Most sales managers waste 4 hours every Friday afternoon stitching CRM data into a deck nobody reads."

"Wastes," "stitching," "nobody reads" — three knife twists.

### Beat 3 — Solution + quality guard
"Tally pulls every Salesforce activity from the week and writes the report in your voice — without you ever opening a spreadsheet."

"Without you ever opening a spreadsheet" = quality guard. ✅

### Beat 4 — How it works
3 steps. Arrows.
"Connect Salesforce → Tally drafts the report by Thursday EOD → You edit, send, done."

### Beat 5 — CTA
Two options:
- A) Direct link: "Try the pilot: tally.so/pilot"
- B) Engagement: "Comment 'Tally' for early access"

Maya has only 2K followers. Engagement-gated would underperform (need more comments to bootstrap reach). Direct link in REPLY tweet is better here. → Pick A.

## Step 6 — Pre-ship audit (12-point checklist)

- ✅ First 10 words contain "Tally" + "AI that writes your weekly sales reports"
- ✅ Problem statement uses "waste," "stitching," "nobody reads" (knife twists)
- ✅ Solution has quality guard ("No spreadsheet")
- ✅ How-it-works is 4 terse arrow steps
- ✅ ONE CTA (try the pilot)
- ✅ Link in REPLY tweet, not main post
- ✅ NO em dashes (used periods)
- ✅ NO "excited to share"
- ✅ Founder voice ("we built", "I worked with")
- ✅ Posted Tue/Wed/Thu 9am PT (planned)
- ✅ Account warmed prior week
- ✅ Cofounder + advisors pinged to repost

→ Ship it.

## Day 2 — Origin thread follow-up (Archetype 2)

Now Maya's launch tweet has some traction. Day 2, she ships the origin thread to deepen the audience:

```
1/ Yesterday I launched Tally. The story behind it took 11 years.

2/ I spent 4 years at Salesforce watching sales managers I respected burn their Fridays writing reports nobody read.

3/ Then I joined a Series B startup as Director of Sales Ops. Same problem. I tried to fix it from the inside. Built Zapier workflows. Hired a contractor to script reports.

4/ Nothing worked. The reports needed judgment, voice, narrative. The data could be pulled — the writing couldn't.

5/ Two things changed in 2024. Salesforce APIs got cleaner. And LLMs got good enough to write in someone's voice from 3 examples.

6/ I quit in November. Built the first version in 6 weeks. First customer was a friend running a 40-person sales team. He shipped his Friday report in 12 minutes instead of 4 hours.

7/ He told 3 friends. They told 3 friends. We've been quiet-piloting with 12 sales orgs since.

8/ Today, Tally is available to anyone. Check the pinned post for the demo.

9/ The next 100 pilots are free for the first month. DM me your CRM stack and I'll set you up myself.
```

**Reply tweet (link):** `Try Tally: tally.so/pilot`

## What this example demonstrates

1. **The formula is a constraint, not a cage.** The 5 beats give Maya structure; her voice fills it.
2. **The same launch ships in 3 forms.** Single tweet (Day 1 X), long LinkedIn post (Day 1 LinkedIn), origin thread (Day 2 X). Each post is a distinct distribution unit.
3. **The video carries the proof.** The post earns the watch; the video closes the deal.
4. **The CTA is calibrated to audience size.** 2K followers → direct link. 50K followers → could try comment-gated.
5. **The pre-ship checklist catches problems.** Without it, Maya might've used "excited to share" or stacked CTAs.
