# FAQ

Questions I get from founders before they launch.

---

## Does this work for consumer apps?

Partially. The 5-beat formula works for any product. The archetype selection is different.

- Consumer apps usually skip the YC-style dev-tool pitch (Archetype 1 with HN title).
- Origin-story threads (Archetype 2) and teardown-reveal threads (Archetype 3) work especially well for consumer because the story carries.
- The "we built this with [AI/constraints/weekend]" shock format (Matt Epstein AI-launch-video shock) is very consumer-friendly.

Corpus examples to study: Cluely (consumer-facing, viral positioning), Bolt.new (prosumer), Lovable (prosumer/consumer).

---

## Does this work for B2C physical products?

Yes, but you're not the target audience. The repo is SaaS/software-focused. The formula still applies but many of the tactics (no link in main post, founder account, etc.) work in B2C too.

For e-commerce specifically, study Icon (in the corpus) — it's the ads-platform archetype that Shown Media and Social Capital Inc use.

---

## What if I don't have a video?

Ship the post anyway. A great post with no video outperforms a mediocre post with a video.

But: the post-video relationship is where most launches get the extra 2-3x. If you have budget for one OR the other, most founders benefit more from budget on the video. The post is cheap.

Represent does launch videos. Most launch videos cost $15K-$50K. If that's out of range, you can still launch well — just lean harder on the post itself and on a founder-shot mobile demo video (no production, just you with your phone).

## Does this work for Product Hunt launches?

Yes, but PH has its own mechanics:
- Comment count in first 6 hours is the critical PH metric
- Your X/LinkedIn launch post should drive people to PH, not to your site, on PH day
- CTA: "upvote on PH" instead of "try the product"
- Schedule: start at 12:01am PT (when PH day flips) — different from the Tue/Wed/Thu 9am PT rule for regular launches

The 5-beat formula applies. The timing doesn't.

## What if my launch is stealth (no fundraise, no product)?

Use the "stealth-emergence" variant (see [Trava](corpus/07-trava.md)). Hook: "After months of building in stealth, I'm excited to introduce X." Replace the fundraise number with a market stat or a traction proof point.

---

## What if I'm launching a second product from an existing company?

Use Archetype 1 (YC-style announcement) but lead with a credibility bridge. Example: "Gamma Sites" from Grant Lee leads with "Making an A+ website shouldn't be hard" + then introduces the new product. Doesn't need to rehash the whole company origin.

---

## Should I launch on LinkedIn or X?

Both. Same week, different days.

- **X launch:** Tuesday or Wednesday. Single tweet + reply with link OR thread.
- **LinkedIn launch:** Thursday (24-48 hours after X). Long-form post.

Don't ship same-day on both — they cannibalize. See [LAUNCH-WEEK-SCHEDULE.md](LAUNCH-WEEK-SCHEDULE.md).

---

## What if my audience is tiny (<500 followers)?

Do the work anyway. A tiny audience done well outperforms a big audience done lazily.

Also:
- Pre-launch: add 50-100 real followers in the 3-4 weeks before by engaging with people in your niche.
- Launch day: DM every friend who might amplify. Ask them directly.
- Post-launch: the customer drips (T+3 to T+6 in the schedule) matter MORE for small audiences because they compound.

---

## Should I use my real name or a brand pseudonym?

Real name. Always. Personal accounts get 2.75x impressions and 14x engagement vs brand accounts.

If you're behind a pseudonym for privacy reasons, pick a consistent identity and treat it as a person, not a brand.

## How do I know if my hook is good?

Use the [hook-sharpener prompt](PROMPT.md#prompt-2--the-hook-sharpener). Paste your hook. Run the 5-point diagnostic.

Or use the 30-second test from [02-hook-patterns.md](references/02-hook-patterns.md):
1. Read it aloud. If you stumble, rewrite.
2. Strip the first 3 words. Still grabs? Those 3 were filler.
3. Could a competitor post the same hook? Sharpen with a number or name.
4. Does it earn tweet 2? If no, the hook didn't promise enough.
5. Does it match the video? Tone mismatch kills conversion.

## What if my launch fails?

Two things:

1. **Post the autopsy.** 2-3 days after the launch, post honestly about what didn't work. Honesty posts outperform celebration posts.
2. **Relaunch in 4-6 weeks.** Most "failed" launches just hit the wrong day, the wrong format, or the wrong hook. Rebuild the post. Try again. The second launch works more often than the first.

---

## What if the repo's advice contradicts my gut?

The repo is the 80% rule. Your gut often knows the 20%.

If you've launched before and something worked against this advice, trust your track record over this repo.

If you've never launched before, trust this repo over your gut.
