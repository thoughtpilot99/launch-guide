# The Launch Week Schedule

Most founders think "launch day = 1 post." That's why launches die.

A real launch is 10+ posts over 14 days. This is the day-by-day schedule I give every founder I work with at Represent.

---

## T-14 days — Setup week

**Goal:** Warm the account. Line up the distribution.

- **Day T-14:** Finalize the video. Get the 60-second cut, the 30-second teaser, the 15-second loop, and the 6-second GIF.
- **Day T-14 to T-7:** 15-20 min a day of REAL engagement on LinkedIn and X. Like posts from people you know. Comment thoughtfully on 5-10 posts a day. DO NOT ask for favors yet.
- **Day T-10:** DM 5-10 friends/allies. "Hey, launching on [DATE]. Mind liking the post if you see it?" No ask beyond that. They'll notice. They'll show up.
- **Day T-7:** Pin a "teaser" post to your profile. Something like "T-7 days. Been building this for [N] months. Can't wait to show you." Low-commitment. Builds anticipation in your followers' feed.
- **Day T-5:** Confirm the launch-day schedule with your team. Every cofounder, employee, advisor repost from their personal account within 2 hours of publish.

---

## T-3 days — Final prep

- **Day T-3:** Draft the launch post using [the 5-beat formula](references/06-renat-5-beat-formula.md). Draft the reply tweet with the link. Draft the first-comment reply (LinkedIn).
- **Day T-2:** Audit the draft against [ANTI-PATTERNS.md](ANTI-PATTERNS.md). Fix every fail.
- **Day T-1:** Schedule the launch post in your scheduler (or set an alarm if posting manually). Share the exact link with your team so they know what to repost.
- **Day T-1 evening:** DO NOT pre-announce on the main feed. Keep powder dry.

---

## T-0 — Launch Day

### Morning of

- **8:30am PT:** Open LinkedIn + X. Spend 15 minutes commenting on posts from people you want to engage back when you launch. This primes the algorithm.
- **8:45am PT:** Final read-through of the launch post. Check the video uploads cleanly. Verify the reply-tweet link works.

### 9:00am PT — PUBLISH

- Hit publish on the MAIN POST (founder account).
- Immediately post the REPLY TWEET with the link.
- On LinkedIn: post the link as the FIRST COMMENT.

### 9:00am-9:05am PT

- Text your team: "Live. Repost from your account now."
- DM the 5-10 allies you warmed up. "Hey, we're live if you want to take a look."

### 9:05am-11:00am PT — ENGAGEMENT SPRINT

**This is the critical window. Clear your calendar.**

- Reply THOUGHTFULLY to every comment. Not "thanks!" — actually engage with what they said.
- RT/like-and-reply every QRT from people who shared.
- DM anyone who shows high interest with a direct link to the product.
- Post a "milestone" reply to your own thread every 30 min (e.g., "Blown away by the response. First 100 signups already. Thank you.")

### Afternoon

- **1:00pm PT:** Reply cycle 2. Catch anyone who engaged between 11am-1pm.
- **3:00pm PT:** Reply cycle 3.
- **Evening:** Post a "wrap-up" reply to your main post. "Day 1 in the books. [Metric]. Thank you all. Here's what's next: [tease Day 2 content]."

## T+1 — Origin thread day

**Goal:** Deepen the audience. Convert the new followers into readers.

- **9:00am PT:** Publish the origin thread on X. Use the [origin-thread template](templates/origin-thread.md).
- Structure: Hook → scene-set → pain discovery → turning point → product reveal → customer proof → vision → platform thesis closer.
- Same engagement sprint rules. First 2 hours blocked.

---

## T+2 — LinkedIn long-post day

**Goal:** Reach the LinkedIn audience that doesn't follow you on X.

- **9:00am PT:** Publish a long-form LinkedIn post. Same underlying story, different register. LinkedIn rewards long.
- Use the [fundraise-long-post template](templates/fundraise-long-post.md) if it's a fundraise. Otherwise adapt the origin-thread structure into prose paragraphs.
- Same engagement sprint rules.

---

## T+3 to T+6 — Customer-proof drip

**Goal:** Sustain momentum with a new data point each day.

- **Day T+3:** Post a customer win. "[Name] signed up yesterday. Here's what they said."
- **Day T+4:** Post a metric update. "Day 4: [N] users, [$X] ARR, [Y] DMs."
- **Day T+5:** Post a behind-the-scenes. "Here's what the shoot day looked like." Or "Here's the prompt we used to generate the video."
- **Day T+6:** Post a teardown. "Here's what worked about our launch. Here's what didn't." Meta-content performs.

---

## T+7 — One-week retrospective

**Goal:** Close the loop. Set up the next wave.

- Post the week-one numbers: signups, revenue, press, inbound DMs.
- Tag and thank the top 5-10 amplifiers (people who RT'd and drove real reach).
- Tease what's next. "Week 2 plan: ship [feature] + open [N] more pilot slots."

---

## T+14 — Second-order-effects post

**Goal:** The launch is still working. Show that.

Two weeks after launch, inbound calls are coming in from people who saw the launch post but needed time to think. This is the moment to post the second-order effects.

- "2 weeks ago we launched. Here's what happened I didn't expect: [VC DM, customer from an industry we didn't target, press hit, etc]."
- This post often outperforms the launch post itself because it proves the launch wasn't just a moment.

---

## Monthly cadence after T+14

Launches aren't events. They're month-0 of a 24-month asset.

- **Month 1:** Case studies of the first 10 customers.
- **Month 3:** "The launch video is 90 days old. It's still generating discovery calls. Here's why."
- **Month 6:** Data-heavy post on revenue / users / compounds since launch.
- **Month 12:** One-year retrospective. The "what the launch actually did" post.

Each of these extends the launch tail. Most founders do none of them. That's the gap.

## Tools I use for this

- **Scheduling:** Typefully (X), Taplio (LinkedIn), Hypefury
- **Analytics:** native platform + Taplio analytics for LinkedIn
- **Coordination:** Slack channel for the launch team to coordinate reposts
- **Warm-up tracking:** a simple Notion page listing who I engaged with each day T-7 to T-0, so I can thank them later

## The honest truth

A single launch post done perfectly will outperform 10 launch posts done poorly.

But 10 launch posts across the launch week done well will outperform a single perfect post by 5-10x.

The repo gives you the formula for each post. This schedule tells you when to ship each one.

Ship the week. Not the day.
