# Anti-Patterns — Do Not Ship

Every launch post that dies at 200 views makes at least 3 of these mistakes.

Use this as a final audit before you publish. Fail any of these, fix before ship.

---

## Hook anti-patterns

### ❌ The throat-clear
- "As a founder, I..."
- "I'm excited to share..."
- "After months of hard work..."
- "Today is a special day for our team..."

Fix: cut everything before the noun. Start with the product, the number, or the specific action.

### ❌ The vague promise
- "Stay tuned."
- "Big news coming."
- "Something special dropping soon."

Fix: tell them what the news IS. Vague posts signal low confidence.

### ❌ The hashtag hook
- "#startup #saas #building"

Fix: never hashtags in the hook position. Acceptable on LinkedIn in the last line. Never on X.

### ❌ The emoji-only hook
- "🚀🚀🚀 WE DID IT"

Fix: emoji support copy. They don't replace it.

### ❌ The apology
- "Sorry for the long absence..."
- "I know I haven't posted in a while..."

Fix: never apologize for time off in a launch post. Act like you've been here the whole time.

### ❌ The listicle tease without structure
- "Here are 7 things..." (then 3 things)

Fix: if you promise N, deliver N.

---

## Body anti-patterns

### ❌ Stacked superlatives
- "Most powerful. Most robust. Most comprehensive. Trusted by the best."

Fix: pick ONE. Back it with a specific number or named customer.

### ❌ Feature-list dump
- 12 bullets of features.

Fix: 3-4 max. Translate each feature to an outcome.

### ❌ "It's not X, it's Y" symmetric rhetoric
- "It's not a tool. It's a movement."
- "We're not building software. We're building a community."

This is AI-tell. Current LLMs overuse this structure. Human founders rarely talk this way.

Fix: just say what it is. Drop the false opposition.

### ❌ "We believe..." paragraphs
- "We believe the future of work is..."

Fix: beliefs don't sell. Proof does. Replace "we believe" with "we just did" or "X customers just did."

### ❌ Em dashes
- "The future — of work — is remote."

Em dashes are AI-tell. Current LLMs overproduce them. Human founders rarely use them in posts.

Fix: periods + line breaks.

### ❌ Consultant transitions
- "Moreover,"
- "Furthermore,"
- "In conclusion,"
- "It's worth noting that..."

Fix: cut them. If a sentence needs "moreover" to connect, the previous sentence wasn't strong enough.

### ❌ "In today's fast-paced world"
- Any variant of this phrase.

Fix: burn it. Never shipped a real launch.

## CTA anti-patterns

### ❌ Stacked CTAs
- "Try the product + follow us + sign up for the newsletter + join Discord + share this post + book a call"

Fix: pick ONE.

### ❌ Link in main post
- Hero tweet with a bit.ly link in it.

Fix: link goes in reply tweet or first comment. X and LinkedIn both suppress posts with external links.

### ❌ "Watch the video 👇"
- Post with embedded video that says "watch the video."

Fix: the embed IS the invitation. Don't narrate it.

### ❌ "Link in bio"
- Multi-step CTA requiring the reader to leave, navigate, find, click.

Fix: put the link where they are. Usually a reply tweet.

### ❌ "Thoughts?" at the end
- "...what do you think?"

Fix: works in thought-leadership posts. NOT in launch posts. Launch posts declare.

### ❌ "RT if you agree"
- Explicit engagement-bait.

Fix: earn the RT with the content. Asking signals weakness.

### ❌ "Tell me what you want to see next"
- Launch post ending with a feedback poll.

Fix: launches aren't polls. Pick a direction. Ship it.

## Platform anti-patterns

### ❌ Launching from the company account
- @companyname posts the launch, founder retweets it.

Fix: invert. Founder posts. Company retweets. Personal accounts get 2.75x impressions and 14x engagement vs brand accounts (per research cited in the 5-beat formula).

### ❌ Wrong day / wrong time
- Launching Friday afternoon.
- Launching Monday at 6am.
- Launching on a federal holiday.

Fix: Tuesday / Wednesday / Thursday at 9am Pacific. Overlaps EU + East Coast + West Coast.

### ❌ Publishing cold
- Post goes live, founder goes to lunch.

Fix: 15 min before publish + 2 hours after = engagement sprint. Reply to every comment.

### ❌ Cold account
- First post in 3 months is the launch.

Fix: warm the account 3-5 days before. 15-20 min a day of real engagement on posts from people you know.

### ❌ The team doesn't share
- Everyone on the team waits for the founder to "make it big."

Fix: every cofounder, employee, advisor reposts from their own personal account within 2 hours of publish.

## Video-post mismatch anti-patterns

### ❌ Video hook ≠ Post hook
- Video: "50 commits beats five commits, right?"
- Post: "🚀 Launching our new product"

Reader sees the post, clicks expecting the product pitch, gets a thought-provoking video, bounces.

Fix: the post's first 7 words should match the video's first 7 words (or be tighter).

### ❌ Wrong video length for post length
- 8-tweet thread wrapping a 15-second teaser clip.
- Single tweet wrapping a 5-minute brand film.

Fix: match energy. 30-60s video → single tweet or long tweet. 2-5min video → thread.

### ❌ External YouTube link as the "video"
- Tweet: "Watch our launch video: [youtube.com link]"

X and LinkedIn suppress external links and external video platforms. The video dies in the feed.

Fix: upload native. If video is >10min, upload a 60-second native teaser that cuts to the longer version via a reply-tweet link.

## Content anti-patterns

### ❌ Fabricated numbers
- "We've helped 10,000 founders launch successfully."
- "Our clients have raised over $100M combined."

Unless sourced, these are a liability.

Fix: use only numbers you can point to.

### ❌ Copying another launch post verbatim
- Stealing a hook line-for-line from someone else's viral post.

Fix: steal the STRUCTURE. Change the nouns. Voice matters.

### ❌ Fabricated credentialing
- "I've been in this industry for 20 years" (when you haven't)
- Listing investors you don't have
- Claiming customers you haven't closed

One fact-check tweet destroys the launch and the account.

---

## The 30-second audit

Before hitting publish, check:

1. First 10 words contain product name + category ✅
2. Problem statement uses a knife-twisting adjective ✅
3. Solution has a quality guard ("without [bad thing]") ✅
4. How-it-works is 3-4 terse steps ✅
5. ONE CTA ✅
6. Link in reply, NOT main post ✅
7. No em dashes ✅
8. No "excited to share" / "stay tuned" ✅
9. Founder account, not company ✅
10. Video hook matches post hook ✅
11. Posted Tue/Wed/Thu 9am PT ✅
12. Account warmed prior 3-5 days ✅

Fail any, fix before ship.

---

## The honest truth

Most founders break 4-6 of these on every launch. That's why most launches die.

Follow the rules. Ship the post. Watch what happens.
