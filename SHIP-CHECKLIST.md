# The Ship Checklist

Print this. Pin it. Tick every box before hitting publish.

If you fail any of these, DO NOT SHIP. Fix it first.

---

## The Post (the 5 beats)

- [ ] **Beat 1 — Announcement.** First 10 words contain product name + category. ("Introducing [X], the first [Y]")
- [ ] **Beat 2 — Problem.** One sentence. Includes an adjective that "twists the knife" (brutally, absurdly, quietly)
- [ ] **Beat 3 — Solution.** One sentence. Includes a "quality guard" ("without [bad thing]")
- [ ] **Beat 4 — How it works.** 3-4 steps in arrows (→) or numbers. Terse.
- [ ] **Beat 5 — CTA.** ONE. Not stacked.

## The Hook

- [ ] First 7 words contain a number, name, or specific noun (no generic "we/I/our")
- [ ] Would not fit on a competitor's account (specific to YOU)
- [ ] Read it aloud. No stumble. No embarrassment.
- [ ] Matches the video's opening line (if video attached)

## The Rules (non-negotiable)

- [ ] Link is in REPLY tweet (or first comment on LinkedIn), NOT main post
- [ ] Posting from FOUNDER account, not company account
- [ ] Scheduled for Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday at 9am Pacific
- [ ] No em dashes anywhere in the post
- [ ] No "excited to share" / "stay tuned" / "blessed to announce"
- [ ] No "it's not X, it's Y" symmetric rhetoric
- [ ] No stacked superlatives ("most powerful, most robust, most comprehensive")
- [ ] No hashtags in the hook position

## The Pre-Launch Warm-Up

- [ ] Account warmed 3-5 days before publish (15-20 min/day of real engagement on posts from people you know)
- [ ] 5-10 allies DM'd about the launch date (with enough time to plan)
- [ ] Team briefed on the repost plan
- [ ] Teaser post pinned to profile T-7 (optional but helps)

## The Video (if embedded)

- [ ] Uploaded NATIVELY (not a YouTube link)
- [ ] First 3 seconds hook the watcher without sound
- [ ] Captions burned in (most feeds mute by default)
- [ ] Video length matches post length (30-60s → single tweet, 60-120s → long post or mini-thread)
- [ ] Post hook matches video hook

## The Launch Day Execution

- [ ] 15 minutes before publish: engage with 10 posts from your network
- [ ] Hit publish at exactly 9am PT (not 8:55, not 9:05)
- [ ] Post the reply tweet with the link IMMEDIATELY after publish
- [ ] Text the team: "Live. Repost now."
- [ ] DM the 5-10 pre-warmed allies: "We're live if you want to take a look"
- [ ] Calendar cleared for 2 hours post-publish for the engagement sprint

## The 2-Hour Engagement Sprint

- [ ] Reply thoughtfully to EVERY comment (no generic "thanks!")
- [ ] RT and reply to every QRT
- [ ] DM high-intent engagers with a direct product link
- [ ] Post a "milestone" reply to your own thread at 30-min intervals
- [ ] Like every comment (signals algorithm)

## Post-Publish (Day-of Wrap-Up)

- [ ] 1pm PT: reply cycle 2 (catch everyone who engaged after the first 2 hours)
- [ ] 3pm PT: reply cycle 3
- [ ] Evening: post a "day 1 wrap-up" reply with a milestone number
- [ ] Tomorrow's origin thread drafted and scheduled

## Day T+1 to T+14 (the launch week is 14 days, not 1)

- [ ] Day T+1: origin thread on X (or primary platform not used day-of)
- [ ] Day T+2: long-form post on LinkedIn
- [ ] Day T+3: customer win post
- [ ] Day T+4: metric update post
- [ ] Day T+5: behind-the-scenes post
- [ ] Day T+6: teardown / meta-content post
- [ ] Day T+7: one-week retrospective with numbers
- [ ] Day T+14: second-order-effects post

---

## The Final Test

Before you hit publish, ask yourself ONE question:

**"If only the first tweet/line got read, does it stand alone?"**

60% of readers will only see tweet 1. If that single unit doesn't:
- Name the product
- Promise a specific outcome
- Earn the watch/click/scroll

...rewrite it.

The rest of the post matters for the 40%. The first line matters for everyone.

---

## If you fail more than 3 boxes

Don't ship. Push the launch by a week. Fix the fails. A great launch is worth delaying. A bad launch you can't undo.

---

## If you pass every box

Ship it. Then execute the 2-hour sprint. Then the 14-day schedule. Then report back.

Launches compound. The more you ship, the better each one gets.

Let's go.
