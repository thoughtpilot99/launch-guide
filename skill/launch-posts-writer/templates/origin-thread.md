# Template — Origin-Story Thread

**Use for:** First-time founder launches. Products where the story > the feature. Fundraise + origin combo posts. LinkedIn long-posts that need to sustain 300+ words.

**Length:** 8-12 tweets OR single long post.

---

## Fill-in skeleton (8-tweet version)

```
Tweet 1 (HOOK):
[SPECIFIC NUMBER/CLAIM + TEASE]. [POST-HOOK WHY-SHOULD-I-READ LINE].

Tweet 2 (SCENE-SET):
[One sentence placing the reader in a specific time/place/moment from your founder journey. Specificity > generality].

Tweet 3 (PAIN DISCOVERY):
[The moment you saw the problem. Include one specific detail (customer name, dollar figure, failure mode)].

Tweet 4 (TURNING POINT):
[What made you build this. One sentence. Quote your co-founder or early user if applicable].

Tweet 5 (PRODUCT REVEAL, with video):
Today we're launching [PRODUCT] — [ONE-LINE SUMMARY]. [VIDEO].

Tweet 6 (PROOF):
[First customer / early user / metric]. Include a named company or first name.

Tweet 7 (VISION):
[Where this goes in 6 months / 2 years. Concrete enough to bet on].

Tweet 8 (CTA + LINK IN REPLY):
[Platform thesis sentence — quotable]. [Reply this tweet with your own launch or DM to [email]].
```

---

## Worked example (Lovable-style opening)

```
Tweet 1:
Today Lovable raised $200M at a $1.8B valuation led by Accel.

I've never shared how it started: I called my friend at 6AM to go for a walk.

Tweet 2:
I was CTO at Depict. We hit $1M ARR faster than any Swedish startup. But something felt off.

Tweet 3:
In 2023, I hacked something together one weekend: 'plain english to working app.' Called it GPT Engineer. Posted on GitHub. Went to sleep.

Tweet 4:
I woke up to chaos. The messages weren't from developers. They were from people who'd never coded before, building their first apps. That's when it clicked.

Tweet 5:
Today we're launching Lovable — the product that builds other products.
[Video]

Tweet 6:
Caio Moretti made $3M in 48 hours with a product built on Lovable. Airweave (agentic search) just got into Y Combinator. Felipe hit $10K MRR with his laundry app.

Tweet 7:
Before Canva, there were only 5M designers. Canva made everyone capable of designing — 44x'd the market to 220M.

Lovable is doing the same for code.

Tweet 8:
This is the age of the builder.

Reply: [waitlist link]
```

## Variants

### LinkedIn long-post version
Collapse the 8 tweets into 8 paragraphs. Keep the same structure. No numbered hashes. Close with the platform thesis + soft CTA ("If you're building, DM me").

### Year-stamped chapters (Gamma-style)
Instead of tweets 2-4 being one narrative, structure as:
```
2020: [What was true then]
2022: [The turning point]
2023: [The breakthrough]
2025: [Current state]
```

### Pivot-origin (Deel-style)
If your story is a pivot, number the tweets 1/–13/ and skip one number mid-thread. The skip is authenticity.

---

## Pre-ship checklist

- [ ] Tweet 1 stands alone (60% of readers only see it)
- [ ] Tweet 2 has a specific detail (name, city, time)
- [ ] Video embedded at tweet 5 (mid-reveal) OR tweet 1
- [ ] Customer proof names 3 specific users with first names + specific metrics
- [ ] Closer is tweet-sized thesis (quotable, shareable)
- [ ] Link in reply tweet, not main thread
- [ ] No em dashes
- [ ] One analogy bridge (category-adjacent, scale-multiplying)
