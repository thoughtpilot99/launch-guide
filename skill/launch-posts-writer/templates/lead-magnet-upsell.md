# Template — Lead Magnet + Service Upsell

**Use for:** Agencies, solo experts, or founders launching a free tool/template/GPT/swipe file where the free thing funnels to a paid service or product.

**Length:** Single tweet on X. Long post on LinkedIn (higher dwell = more comments = algorithmic boost).

**Real anchors:** Icon's 1,000 winning ads giveaway (the rosetta stone, ~10K comments). Matt Epstein's "Mom Test (steal it)" (50M views across launch videos). Renat's Launch GPT + Launch Checklist giveaways.

---

## The mechanic

```
1. Lead with the FREE thing — shock value + specificity
2. Show value in 1-2 sentences
3. ONE sentence credibility ("built from X", "used by Y")
4. Service plug in the LAST SENTENCE (never middle)
5. Comment-gated CTA OR direct link
```

**Why "last sentence for the service plug":** reader who got the free thing AND wants your service has already committed to reading. A service line at the end reads as a soft "by the way." A service line in the middle reads as salesy. Readers bounce.

## Matt Epstein "(steal it)" variant

```
This year [MY THING] did [BIG NUMBER] [RESULT].

Every single [PART] was [UNEXPECTED SOURCE].

I call it [NAMED FRAMEWORK] (steal it).

[Attached: framework cheat card or screenshot]
```

**Why this works:** the named-framework-plus-generosity move. Readers screenshot and share. The framework name becomes a meme. The service sells itself when people ask "who came up with this?"

---

## The upsell pyramid

Layer the offer like this:

```
FREE         → Lead magnet (this post's asset)
              ↓ captures email / follow / comment
LOW-COST    → $99-$499 one-time (template pack, mini-course, audit)
              ↓ qualifies serious buyers
HIGH-TICKET → $5K+ service / retainer
```

The launch post promotes the FREE layer. The free asset promotes the LOW-COST. The low-cost introduces the HIGH-TICKET. Don't skip layers.

---

## Pre-ship checklist

- [ ] First line names the free asset with a specific number
- [ ] 3-5 bullets list what's inside (each with a number)
- [ ] "(Steal it)" or equivalent generosity tag
- [ ] Service plug is in the LAST sentence, not middle
- [ ] Comment-gated CTA ("Comment X") OR direct link (in reply)
- [ ] Follow-required for DM (so follows compound)
- [ ] Link goes in DM or reply, NOT main post
- [ ] No em dashes
- [ ] Posted Tue/Wed/Thu 9am PT
- [ ] Warm the account 3-5 days before
- [ ] Reply to every comment within 2 hours

---

## Cooldown

**Don't reuse this format more than once per 14-21 days on the same account.** Readers notice when every third post is "comment X for the free thing." Burns trust and algorithmic favor.

Matt Epstein uses his "Mom Test (steal it)" pattern ~2x/month. Icon's comment-gated CTA on launch day was a single use. Space them out.
