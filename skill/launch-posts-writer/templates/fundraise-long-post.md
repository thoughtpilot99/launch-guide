# Template — Fundraise Long Post (Osika / Deel / Gamma archetype)

**Use for:** Series A / B / C / Seed round announcements where the raise is the hook and the founder wants to use the moment to tell the full origin story.

**Length:** Long post (single unit), 400-800 words on X Premium OR full LinkedIn long-form.

---

## Fill-in skeleton

```
Today [COMPANY] raised $[X] at a $[Y] valuation led by [LEAD VC].

[1-LINE HIDDEN-STORY TEASE. EG: "I've never shared this before because of how embarrassing our start was."]

[COFOUNDER NAME] and I [SPECIFIC ORIGIN DETAIL — time, place, personal background].

[FIRST IDEA — 1 paragraph. What we originally tried.]

[FIRST FAILURE — 1 paragraph. The thing that didn't work, with a specific dollar figure or user count.]

[PIVOT MOMENT — the customer / user / moment that changed things. Include a verbatim quote if possible.]

[PRODUCT LADDER — 3-4 sentences on how you built from there. Include dates and ARR figures.]

[CUSTOMER PROOF TRIPLE — 3 named customers with first name + specific metric]

[MARKET ANALOGY — "Before Canva, there were only 5M designers..." scale-expanding analogy that reframes the opportunity.]

[CURRENT STATE — one paragraph. Users. Revenue. Team size. Profitability if applicable.]

[CLOSER — tweet-sized platform thesis. Quotable. Shareable.]
```

---

## Worked structure (Deel $300M Series E)

```
Deel has raised $300M at a $17.3B valuation.

Shuo and I started about 6 years ago, struggling to even make $10K.

I've never shared this before because of how embarrassing our start was.

1/ [Founder background — outsiders in Silicon Valley, parents emigrated from North Africa]

2/ [Original idea — escrow platform for freelancer payments]

3/ [First failure — pitched Reddit freelancers, nobody cared. The crushing realization.]

4/ [Pivot hell — specific anecdote about recovering $7K from $15K invoice]

5/ [False PMF]

6/ [The pivot-triggering customer quote]

7/ [Decision to pivot]

8/ [10-day traction to Demo Day]

10/ [Ongoing customer channel — WhatsApp, Slack]
(skip 9 — human touch)

11-12/ [Product ladder: Employer of Record → Payroll]

13/ [Current state: $1B ARR, first $100M revenue month]

All good things start small and shaky.
```

---

## Archetype switches

### Stealth-emergence (no fundraise to announce)
Replace hook 1 with: "After months of building in stealth, I'm excited to introduce [Product]." Continue with same structure. Use a market-stat instead of a dollar figure for credibility. (See Trava.)

### "We grew to $X faster than [Y, Z]" (Alex Bouaziz style)
Hook: "We grew to $[X] ARR faster than Stripe, Salesforce, and Palantir, while being 100% remote." Then 3-5 principles-based paragraphs. Post is educational, not narrative.

### "Gamma didn't start with AI" (Grant Lee style)
Year-stamped chapters instead of numbered tweets. 2020 → 2022 → 2023 → 2025 formatting. Each year = one specific scene.

---

## Signature lines to steal (customize the noun)

- "This is the age of the [X]." (Osika)
- "All good things start small and shaky." (Bouaziz)
- "Aim [the chip] in the right direction." (Grant Lee)
- "If you've [failed/succeeded/stumbled] before, don't try to get rid of the [scar/chip/memory]."

Do NOT clone verbatim. Customize the noun to your thesis.

---

## Pre-ship checklist

- [ ] Opens with dollar figure + valuation + lead VC
- [ ] Immediately follows with a hidden-story tease
- [ ] Includes at least one specific EARLY dollar figure ($7K, $10K, $1M ARR)
- [ ] Names the cofounder with a scene (walk / call / meeting)
- [ ] Pivot moment includes a verbatim customer quote
- [ ] Customer proof triple: 3 first names + 3 specific metrics + 3 specific outcomes
- [ ] One market analogy (scale-multiplying)
- [ ] Close is a tweet-sized thesis (quotable)
- [ ] No em dashes
- [ ] No stacked CTAs
- [ ] Image: team photo or milestone graphic. NOT a demo video.
