# Cube — "I've been saying dashboards are dying"

## Verbatim post

> I've been saying dashboards are dying. Not because visualization is dead, but because static reports can't keep up with how data evolves. The future is agentic analytics: AI agents that don't just answer questions, but build data models from scratch, generate insights, and run autonomous analytics workflows. Everything in my career has been building toward this (text-to-SQL at FriendlyData -> AI-first analytics at Narrative BI -> Agentic Analytics) and that's why I joined Cube. We just launched Cube Agentic Analytics – the first agentic analytics platform built on a semantic layer. AI agents (and humans) can query, explore, and manipulate data models through natural language. No hallucinations. No inconsistent metrics. Just governed, reliable analytics that scales. Dashboards aren't obsolete. They're just becoming intelligent.

## Pattern analysis

**Hook:** "I've been saying dashboards are dying." — provocation + self-reference. Rare opener — "I've been saying" positions the writer as someone who has been claiming this before the mainstream.

**Structural beats:**
1. Provocation ("X is dying")
2. Immediate qualifier ("not because Y, but because Z")
3. Category claim ("agentic analytics")
4. Career arc credentialing (FriendlyData → Narrative BI → Cube)
5. Product reveal + "first" claim
6. 3-bullet differentiators (no hallucinations / no inconsistent metrics / governed)
7. Reconciling close ("not obsolete, just intelligent")

**Signature tics:** Provocation-then-qualify. Career arrow (`→`). No em dashes. Paradox close ("aren't obsolete, just becoming intelligent").

**Why it underperformed:** Modest engagement (44 reactions). Likely because: (a) thesis is abstract without concrete proof, (b) no specific customer/number in the body, (c) "agentic analytics" as category feels like one of many such phrases in 2025.

**Lesson:** Provocation hooks work, but need backup proof in the body. "Dashboards are dying" + no customer data = smart take, no spread. Compare to Trava which also provocates but backs with $200B stat.

**Lessons for the skill:**
- Provocation openers ("X is dying") need a concrete proof point in the body.
- Career-arrow credential line is clean and reusable: `[Company A] → [Company B] → [Company C]`.
- Paradox close ("not obsolete, just becoming intelligent") is a reliable reframe move.
