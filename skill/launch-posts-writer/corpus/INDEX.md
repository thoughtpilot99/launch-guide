## Cross-pattern quick-reference

**Strongest hooks in the corpus:**
1. "AI agents will pay you to chat with them." (Humwork — inversion)
2. "I got kicked out of Columbia for building an AI that cheats on coding interviews." (Cluely — consequence-as-credential)
3. "AI launch videos did 50M+ views. Every script was written by my 61-year-old mother." (Matt Epstein — absurd contrast)
4. "I've been saying dashboards are dying." (Cube — provocation, but needs proof backup)
5. "Today Lovable raised $200M at a $1.8B valuation led by Accel." (Osika — blunt number)

**Strongest closers in the corpus:**
1. "This is the age of the builder." (Osika)
2. "All good things start small and shaky." (Bouaziz)
3. "Aim [the chip] in the right direction." (Grant Lee)
4. "We're helping everyone realize they have things to build." (Osika)
5. "Cluely is the bridge to a world where humans don't compete with machines. We grow with them." (Cluely)

**Strongest analogies:**
1. "ChatGPT + CapCut, but for winning ads" (Icon)
2. "Waymo had 20M miles on real roads, but 20B miles in sim" (Snowglobe)
3. "Before Canva, there were only 5M designers. Canva 44x'd the market to 220M." (Lovable)
4. "[Existing thing] doesn't work well with [new domain]" (Replicate — the wedge sentence)

**Signature moves worth stealing:**
- Skipped-number in a numbered thread (Deel — human touch)
- "(steal it)" parenthetical (Matt Epstein — weaponized generosity)
- Tri-segmented CTA (Humwork — marketplaces)
- Ratio invention ("$2M ARR per employee" — Gamma)
- Year-stamped chapters (Grant Lee — pacing)
- Consequence-as-credential (Cluely — "kicked out of Columbia")
- Customer-proof triple with first names + specific metrics (Lovable)
- Comment-gated CTA on launch day only (Crunched — 2.4K comments)
