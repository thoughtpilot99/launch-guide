## Pattern analysis

**Hook:** "AI agents will pay you to chat with them." — **strongest hook in the corpus.** 8 words. Inversion (humans pay AI → AI pays humans). Future tense punch with present-tense proof immediately after.

**Structural beats:**
1. Inversion hook (1 sentence)
2. Mechanism in one sentence (MCP server, 30 seconds)
3. "80% then loops" pain enumeration
4. "Live today" — urgency
5. Tri-segmented CTA (devs / companies / experts)

**Signature tics:**
- Wave emoji opener (👋) — friendly
- "80% of the way there, then loops" — memorable failure-mode phrase
- Tri-audience CTA (devs / companies / experts) — segments the read
- Founder email as direct CTA (founders@humwork.ai)

**Why the inversion hook works:** Most launch hooks are "we built X." This is "X already happened, you just hadn't noticed." Future-as-present framing. Reframes a marketplace product as inevitability.

**Lessons for the skill:**
- Inversion = highest-leverage hook archetype if the product naturally inverts an existing relationship.
- Tri-segmented CTAs work for marketplaces (sellers + buyers + supply).
- Naming specific failure modes ("loops on the same bug, makes the same bad architectural guess five times") earns trust faster than feature lists.
- "Live today" + "in under a minute" is a faster activation framing than "join the waitlist".
