## Pattern analysis

**Hook:** "As a founder, I always felt building products takes too long." — personal-pain opener.

**Follow-up pattern:** The $4M/4-week and $40M/5-month posts use the **SAME sentence structure**: "Lovable is the fastest growing startup in Europe ever, $[N] ARR in [N] [weeks/months]."

**Why the repetition works:** Identical sentence = brand mnemonic. Readers recognize the template. Each new post reinforces the pattern. "Fastest growing startup in Europe ever" becomes Lovable's signature phrase.

## Lessons for the skill

- **Sentence templates across posts build brand mnemonic.** Osika uses "Lovable is the fastest growing startup in Europe ever, $X ARR in Y [time]" as a recurring sentence. Find your template.
- **UGC contests via brand account** are cheap distribution. $8K prize = thousands of QRTs.
- **Customer-revenue proof posts ("Lovable-built app just made $3M")** are post archetype worth repeating every time a customer hits a milestone. It's social proof AND repeat distribution.
- **Personal-pain openers ("As a founder, I always felt")** work for solo technical founders. Less effective for agencies/teams.
