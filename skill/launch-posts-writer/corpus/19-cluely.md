# Cluely — "I got kicked out of Columbia"

## Verbatim post (via Renat's video deconstruction)

> I got kicked out of Columbia for building an AI that cheats on coding interviews. Now, we raised $5.3 million to build Cluely, a cheating tool for literally everything.
>
> A completely undetectable AI that sees your screen, hears your audio, and gives you real-time assistance in any situation. Interviews, assessments, tests, sales calls, meetings, everything.
>
> And I don't think it's cheating.
>
> Every single time technology has made people smarter, the world panics. [...] But there's a difference. Humanity is at the inflection point. AI will transform the entire world and will be more disruptive than anything we've ever seen.
>
> Cluely is the bridge to a world where humans don't compete with machines. We grow with them.

## Pattern analysis (per Renat's deconstruction)

**Hook:** "I got kicked out of Columbia for building an AI that cheats on coding interviews." — 14-word consequence-as-credential. Getting kicked out reads as proof of commitment to the idea.

**Structural beats:**
1. Consequence-as-credential hook ("kicked out of Columbia")
2. Current state ("raised $5.3M to build")
3. Provocative positioning ("cheating tool for literally everything")
4. Product description (undetectable, sees/hears, 5 specific use cases)
5. Reframe ("And I don't think it's cheating.")
6. Manifesto ("Every single time technology has made people smarter, the world panics...")
7. Close ("bridge to a world where humans don't compete with machines. We grow with them.")

## Why Renat flags this as a masterclass

**The positioning:** *"There are millions of AI agents that help you do things on your computer — sales calls, note-taking, all of it. Instead, they position it as a cheating tool, and then they reframe things like 'we need to be embracing technology.' Very different from your traditional AI assistant for X. Great differentiation."*

**The video:** a dating-interview where AI helps the candidate. Relatable, high-stakes, not the usual LinkedIn tech-bro content. The video did as much heavy lifting as the post.

## Lessons for the skill

- **Consequence-as-credential** is a high-leverage hook pattern. Getting kicked out / sued / banned / fired for the thing you're building > traditional credentials. Only works if the consequence is real and true.
- **Provocative positioning** (cheating tool) requires immediate reframe ("I don't think it's cheating"). The provocation earns the read; the reframe earns the share.
- **Manifesto close** works when the product's real positioning is a worldview, not a feature set.
- **Video-post alignment:** The dating interview video has the same register as the post — provocative, stakes-high, zoomer-coded. Tone-matching between video and post doubles both.

**Warning:** the "kicked out of Columbia" hook only works once. Don't clone it. Find your own consequence-as-credential.
