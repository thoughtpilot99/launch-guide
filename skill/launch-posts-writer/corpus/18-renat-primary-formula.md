# Renat's Launch-Post Formula (Primary Source)

**Renat's opening frame:** "I've analyzed over 300 launch posts across LinkedIn and X. The formula is very clear. You always need to have a great written launch post AND a video. There wasn't a single post that didn't have a fantastic video."

**The relationship:** post + video are two components. This skill covers the written portion. The video is produced separately (Represent Studio or equivalent).

---

## The 5-beat formula (Renat's verbatim teaching)

### Beat 1 — Clear announcement, no fluff
**Renat:** *"Announce what we're building without fluff. 'Introducing ICON, the world's first AI admaker' — just the name and what it does in the easiest way to understand. Definitely do not complicate this part."*

**Pattern:** `Introducing [Product], the world's first [Category].` — or its variants.

### Beat 2 — Problem statement (twist the knife)
**Renat:** *"The problem that we're solving. 'Making lots of ads is extremely painful.' The more relatable this pain is going to be, the better your post is going to do. Consider using some adjectives just to twist the knife how they say it in copywriting to make people feel the actual pain or recall the pain that they experienced in the past."*

**Craft note:** "Twist the knife" = adjectives that activate memory of pain. Not "it's slow" — "it's brutally slow, and you've been stuck on the same model for 6 hours."

### Beat 3 — Solution (how product solves it)
**Renat:** *"How your product solves the problem. For example, 'Icon remixes your existing footage into new ads matching your production quality.' You're going to get tons and tons of new combinations without losing the quality."*

**Craft note:** One sentence. What it does + what you get + the quality guard ("without losing [X]").

### Beat 4 — How it works (quick numbered list)
**Renat:** *"You can have a quick numbered list. Again, Icon could have done better — it was a little too wordy. A better version: 'Icon tags your video library → generates scripts using a GPT → matches clips to scenes → then you can edit it very fast.'"*

**Craft note:** 3-4 steps, one line each. Arrows (→) or numbers. Terse > wordy.

### Beat 5 — Call to action (frictionless OR engagement tactic)
**Renat:** *"It needs to be absolutely frictionless. You can tell people to check out directly, or you can use an engagement tactic. [...] Personally, I like the engagement technique more because we can build a lead list, we can retarget people, and we can expect that some portion of their audience are also going to see it."*

## Renat's HARD RULES (verbatim constraints)

1. **DO NOT put the link in the launch post.** *"LinkedIn is going to not promote your post organically as much if you have a link to the post in the post. Then X will do the same. What you need to do instead is put it in the follow-up tweet or in the comment. Alternatively, you can also disguise the link by using like dot in the curly brackets."*

2. **Post from founder's account, NOT company account.** *"100% from the founder's account. There's some research from Refined Labs that found that personal LinkedIn profiles drive about 2.75x more impressions and 5 times more engagement. [...] Valytics also did an analysis of 400 million LinkedIn impressions and confirmed that employee posts get 14x more engagement. So there's no reason to make a post on your company account. What you want to do instead is you want all of your employees or all of the team members to repost your main X or LinkedIn post and share their thoughts."*

3. **Timing: Tuesday / Wednesday / Thursday at 9am PT.** *"Weekend, definitely not. Mondays, you have all of your planning calls. Fridays, the mind is absolutely somewhere else. [...] The best time is usually around 9:00 a.m. Pacific time because it overlaps with different time zones — Europe, East Coast, West Coast."*

4. **Warm up the account 3-5 days before launch.** *"Spend around 15 to 20 minutes every day going through your feed and liking posts and commenting. The best would be to engage with posts from people you know, because when you do go live, those people are going to be the first ones to see your post and they're going to pay you back the favor."*

5. **Engagement sprint in the first 2 hours.** *"15 minutes before you hit that publish button, you want to do exactly the same. The next hour or the next 2 hours, you want to be constantly monitoring and responding to people's comments. Ideally something a little more thoughtful that will get folks to re-engage."*

## Renat's 3 deconstructed examples (what he taught)

### Example 1 — Icon (~10K comments, tens of millions of views)
- **Hook:** "Introducing Icon, the world's first AI admaker." (clear category claim)
- **Social proof:** "Backed by Peter Thiel's Foundation"
- **Positioning sentence:** "Icon is like ChatGPT + CapCut, but for winning ads with AI in minutes." (Renat: "Absolutely love this. People already familiar. When you introduce a completely new category, people need to compare it to something.")
- **How it works:** 4 steps (Renat's note: "could be simplified — sentences shorter and more to the point")
- **Outcome frame:** "3-person creative team → 30 ads/month. Icon → 300 ads/month." (10x clarity)
- **Social proof brands:** Jones Road, Ridge, etc. (Renat: "probably more for investors than anybody else")
- **Price wedge:** "Only $400/month" (vs competitors at $2K-$30K)
- **Engagement tactic:** "Drop a comment [keyword] to get 1,000 winning ads." → 10K comments.

### Example 2 — Snowglobe (~3K likes, 300 comments on LinkedIn — Represent client)
- **Clear announcement:** "Today, we're announcing Snowglobe, the simulation engine for AI chatbots."
- **Problem:** "Blind spots where your chatbots fail."
- **Analogy:** Self-driving cars test billions of scenarios in simulation. (Renat: "This especially visible in the video itself. This is a combination of the launch post and the video.")
- **Human component:** "Treya has faced an issue of not having enough training data." (Renat: "Makes her stand out from companies coming out of college dorm rooms trying to build the next AI for X.")

### Example 3 — Cluely (~6K+ likes, viral)
- **Hook:** "I got kicked out of Columbia for building an AI that cheats on coding interviews. Now, we raised $5.3 million to build Cluely."
- **Social proof:** $5.3M raise
- **Product description:** "a completely undetectable AI that sees your screen, hears your audio, and gives you real-time assistance in any situation. Interviews, assessments, tests, sales calls, meetings, everything."
- **Manifesto close:** "And I don't think it's cheating. Every time technology has made people smarter, the world panics. [...] Cluely is the bridge to a world where humans don't compete with machines. We grow with them."
- **Renat's note:** *"My absolute favorite thing about Cluely is their positioning. Millions of AI agents help you do things on your computer. Instead, they position it as a cheating tool. [...] Very different from traditional AI assistant for X. Great differentiation."*
- **Video:** Dating interview scene (viral, relatable, high-stakes, different from LinkedIn/tech-Twitter norm).

## How this file drives the skill

When writing a launch post:
1. Open the 5-beat formula (above) and fill each.
2. Apply the HARD RULES (no link in post, founder account, Tue/Wed/Thu 9am PT, warm-up, 2-hour engagement sprint).
3. Pull an analogy from a category the audience already trusts (like Icon's "ChatGPT + CapCut" or Snowglobe's "self-driving cars").
4. Include the founder's human component (Treya's origin, Kennan's Columbia story).
5. Choose engagement CTA (comment-gated) OR direct-link CTA — not both.
6. Draft link goes in the REPLY, never the main post.
