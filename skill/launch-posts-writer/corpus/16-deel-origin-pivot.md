# Deel — "$300M at $17.3B" + The Pivot Story

## Verbatim post (LinkedIn)

> Deel has raised $300M at a $17.3B valuation Shuo and I started about 6 years ago, struggling to even make $10K I've never shared this before because of how embarrassing our start was.
>
> 1/ Shuo and I were in completely different worlds. She was in China, I was in Israel. Both my parents family emigrated from North Africa with nothing. One of my grandfathers used to work at the French subway and the other was an electrician. I grew up in a entrepreneurial family, but when we arrived in Silicon Valley, we were outsiders.
>
> 2/ Our original idea was to ensure freelancers get paid and not ghosted after delivering work. We built an escrow platform where money would be locked upfront and automatically released when milestones were completed.
>
> 3/ We pitched Reddit freelancers our milestone-based contracts. Nobody cared. The crushing realization: contractors don't choose payment methods, clients do.
>
> 4/ We entered pivot hell. I started being a collection agency for freelancers using our product sending legal letters to companies We recovered $7,000 from a $15,000 invoice after my email. The freelancer gave us $1,000. That was a huge WIN.
>
> 5/ We thought we'd found product market fit. But we never collected another dollar.
>
> 6/ Then a YC batchmate from Sunsama used our product differently. "I like tying contracts to payments and having payment happen as work gets done," he said. "Plus you're giving me contracts out of the box and a way to pay internationally."
>
> 7/ We listened to the customer and pivoted to a contracts product.
>
> 8/ Within ten days, on Demo Day we had 290 contractors, $5,000 monthly revenue. This business only scaled to +$100M.
>
> 10/ Throughout this time, I've had hundreds of customers on WhatsApp and Slack. (I still do this) Our customers told us what their bigger problems were. We listened.
>
> 11/ We launched Employer of Record - Deel would put people on its own payroll on behalf of the client and handle all compliance. This became a $300M ARR+ business.
>
> 12/ We then launched Payroll - a system to automatically do tax calculations and remit the correct tax and net salary to employees globally. This became a $150M+ business.
>
> 13/ Deel was a remote only company and as Deel grew it became obvious to us what problems companies face running global teams. So, we built Deel for Deel. In the last 18 months, we launched 6 more products, hit $1bn ARR and September was our first $100M revenue month.
>
> All good things start small and shaky.

## Pattern analysis

**Hook:** "Deel has raised $300M at a $17.3B valuation" + immediate "I've never shared this before because of how embarrassing our start was."

**Structural beats (13 numbered tweets):**
1. Founder background (outsiders in Silicon Valley)
2. Original idea
3. First failure ("nobody cared")
4. "Pivot hell" + specific dollar anecdote ($7K recovered, $1K fee, "huge WIN")
5. False PMF
6. The pivot-triggering customer conversation (verbatim quote)
7. The decision to pivot
8. First 10-day traction ($5K MRR)
9. Skipped number (note: 9 is missing in verbatim)
10. Ongoing customer channel (WhatsApp, Slack)
11-12. Product ladder (Employer of Record → Payroll)
13. Current state ($1bn ARR)
14. Closer: "All good things start small and shaky."

**Signature tics:**
- **Skipped number (10 → 11, no 9)** — Alex numbers his tweets 1-13 but skips 9. Human. Imperfect. Adds authenticity.
- **"Pivot hell"** — coined phrase for the founder's-purgatory moment.
- **Specific dollar anecdote** ($7K recovered, $1K fee, "huge WIN") — the embarrassment is specific, which makes the later $1B believable.
- **Verbatim customer quote** — "I like tying contracts to payments..." — the pivot was triggered by one sentence from one customer.
- **Close:** "All good things start small and shaky." — quotable. Shareable. Plants the post as a founder-wisdom artifact.

**Why it works (14K reactions):**
- The pivot story is the hook, not the raise.
- Embarrassment + specificity = trust.
- Skipped numbering = humanness.
- Closer is a tweet-sized thesis others will repost.

**Contrasted with Deel's earlier launches:**
- $1M → $100M ARR (Apr 2022): emoji-heavy short tweet. Pure milestone flex. Works because audience was already there.
- $1B ARR reflection (Oct 2025): long post with principles. Educational.
- $300M Series E (Oct 2025): origin-pivot thread. Emotional.

**Lesson:** The same founder used three different archetypes at three different stages. Short flex when audience is already won; educational when founder is established; emotional origin when the company has a big story to justify.

## Lessons for the skill

- **Pivot stories are a first-class launch archetype.** When there's a genuine pivot, it IS the story.
- **Specificity of the embarrassing early dollar** ($7K recovered, $1K fee) is what makes the $1B ARR believable.
- **Verbatim customer quotes** carry weight thread quotes and "we heard from a user" paraphrases cannot.
- **Number every tweet in a long thread — and skip one.** Looks human. The miss is a signal, not a bug.
- **End with a tweet-sized thesis.** "All good things start small and shaky." This is what gets screenshot.
