## Pattern analysis

**Hook:** "10x the context of your coding agent" + numeric subhead "Improved Cursor's performance by 27%" — the 27% is the workhorse: concrete, benchmarkable, against a brand devs already know.

**Structural beats:**
1. 10x headline (multiplier hook)
2. Specific benchmark vs. named brand (Cursor)
3. Category claim ("Context is the bottleneck")
4. Mechanic in one sentence
5. Triple CTA (try / demo / email founder direct)

**Signature tics:** "10x" headline (Hormozi-coded), founder email in CTA (lowers reply barrier), "just know" in scare quotes, declarative category-claim ("X is solved. Y is the bottleneck.").

**Why founder email works:** for technical SaaS targeting devs, an email-the-founder CTA reads as accessible and high-signal. Lifts reply rate vs. landing-page-only CTAs.

**Lessons for the skill:**
- For benchmark-able products, lead with the benchmark vs. a brand the audience already trusts.
- "Code generation is solved. Context is the bottleneck." = perfect category-narrowing hook. Apply pattern: "[Old problem] is solved. [New problem] is the bottleneck."
- Triple-CTA segmented by intent (try / book / email) outperforms a single CTA for high-consideration products.
