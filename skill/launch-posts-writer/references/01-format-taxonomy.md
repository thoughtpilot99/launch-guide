# Launch Post Format Taxonomy

The launch post is the **distribution wrapper for the launch video**. The video is the anchor asset. The post sets up the watch, frames the moment, and funnels attention to the CTA (product signup, waitlist, discovery call, free resource → service upsell).

Every launch post fits one of these six archetypes. Pick the archetype **before** writing a word.

---

## Archetype 1 — The YC-Style Launch Announcement (single tweet)

**Used when:** Founder has a live demo/product, small audience, needs a dense hook.
**Video role:** Native 30-60s product video embedded. Auto-plays in feed.
**Length:** 1 tweet, 240-280 chars.
**Structure beats:**
1. Opener = product category + name (first 5 words)
2. One-line problem statement
3. One-line solution with specificity (metric or verb)
4. Link to site / YC page
5. "Try it: [URL]"

**Example skeleton:**
> `Today, we're launching [Product] — [one-line what it does].`
> `[Problem it solves, 1 line].`
> `[Concrete differentiator, 1 line].`
> `Try it: [URL]`
> `(video attached)`

**Why it works:** Low-friction. Scrollable. Video does the heavy lifting. Hook is the product name itself. Works in YC batch surge days when attention is fragmented.

**Real anchors:** Crunched (YC S25), Nozomio (YC S25), most YC Demo Day companies.

---

## Archetype 2 — The Origin-Story Thread

**Used when:** Founder has a story (unusual background, unlikely pivot, visible struggle) that earns attention. Used when story > feature.
**Video role:** Embedded at tweet 1 OR held to reveal tweet in middle OR pinned as payoff.
**Length:** 6-12 tweets.
**Structure beats:**
1. **Hook tweet** — concrete specific claim or number ("$6M in revenue. 0 employees. Here's the story.")
2. **Scene-set** — 2-3 tweets of origin/problem discovery
3. **Turning point** — what made the founder build this thing
4. **Product reveal** — with video embedded
5. **Proof beat** — what users/customers/investors said
6. **Future frame** — where this goes
7. **CTA** — try it, waitlist, DM for access

**Signature hooks:**
- "I started a US company from 5,424km away. Today we're launching [X]." (distance/constraint)
- "3 years ago I was [humble start]. Today, [X]." (before/after)
- "I [dropped out / got fired / built alone] to build this." (sacrifice narrative)

**CTA variants:**
- Soft: "Reply if you want early access."
- Hard: "We have 50 pilot slots. Link in bio."
- Service-plug: "The launch video was made by [agency]. DM them."

**Real anchors:** Lovable, Linear, Cal.com origin threads.

---

## Archetype 3 — The Viral Teardown / Reveal Thread

**Used when:** Product is technically impressive and the **process** is as interesting as the outcome. Also used for AI-era launches where the "how we built it" is part of the pitch.
**Video role:** Split between teaser (tweet 1) + full reveal (mid-thread).
**Length:** 8-15 tweets.
**Structure beats:**
1. **Teaser hook** — impossible-sounding claim ("We built [X] in [absurd time] with [absurd constraint].")
2. **Stakes** — why it matters / why people said it couldn't be done
3. **Process/tech walk** — 3-5 tweets of build details (screenshots, code, diagrams)
4. **Reveal video** — "Here's the final result:" + embedded
5. **Numbers/metrics** — uptime, performance, scale
6. **Team credit** — name names (this is where agencies get tagged)
7. **What's next**
8. **CTA** — product link

**Signature hooks:**
- "We built [thing] in [time]. Here's how." (process)
- "I made this launch video with [N] prompts. Creatives are cooked." (Matt Epstein pattern)
- "We just replaced [expensive thing] with [cheap thing]." (disruption)

**Video-integration trick:** Tease video in tweet 1 as thumbnail, full play in tweet 5 or 6. Rewards scroll.

**Real anchors:** PJ Ace AI-ad drops, Devin launch thread, Cursor/Zed dev-tool launches.

## Archetype 4 — The Series A / Fundraise Announcement Thread

**Used when:** Company has raised, milestone is funding, launch video is the celebration asset.
**Video role:** Brand / mission film. Less product-heavy, more story-heavy.
**Length:** 5-10 tweets.
**Structure beats:**
1. **Lead metric** — "$X raised at $Y valuation" or "Y customers" or "Z growth"
2. **Company one-liner** — what we do, one sentence
3. **Journey compression** — 3-4 tweets on the path
4. **Investor credit** — named VCs, angels, existing customers-turned-investors
5. **Mission video** embedded
6. **Team shout-out**
7. **"We're hiring"** — almost always
8. **CTA** — customer signup OR careers page

**Signature hooks:**
- "Today, [Company] raised $[X]." (blunt, confident)
- "Two years ago we had 0 customers. Today, [milestone]." (compression)
- "I'm thrilled to share: [Company] just closed $[X] led by [VC]." (LinkedIn register)

**Upsell angle for agencies:** This is the archetype where a brand film gets budget. Post-launch, the agency gets referenced when other founders ask "who made that?"

**Real anchors:** Notion's Series C, Figma pre-Adobe announcements, Linear's Series B.

## Archetype 5 — The "Free Resource" Launch (lead-magnet + service funnel)

**Used when:** Agency or solo expert is launching a free tool/template/GPT/guide — and the product is actually the agency behind it. Classic bait-and-upsell.
**Video role:** Explainer / walkthrough of the free resource. 30-60s.
**Length:** 1-3 tweets (single or mini-thread).
**Structure beats:**
1. **Shock-value hook** — "Absolutely fucked that this is free" / "I can't believe I'm giving this away" / "This would sell for $500"
2. **What it is** — 1 line + link
3. **Why it works** — quick credibility beat (built on Xk data points, used by Y clients, generated $Z)
4. **CTA** — like-and-comment gate OR direct link

**Matt Epstein's signature format (6 uses, top 80,848 engagement / 3.7M views):**
> `Absolutely fucked that this is free`
> `[Link]`
> `+ screenshot of the resource`

**The upsell mechanic:**
- Free resource captures email/like/follow
- Video inside the resource (or the resource itself) demonstrates agency capability
- 10-15% of downloads → discovery call / service inquiry

**Signature hooks (safe variants):**
- "I spent 6 months building this. It's free."
- "[Framework] we use internally. Link."
- "The [X] template our clients pay $[Y] for. Open link."

**Cooldown:** 14-21 days between uses per account or the format burns out.

**Real anchors:** Matt Epstein lead magnets, Launch GPT giveaways.

## Archetype 6 — The Agency/Ghostwriter Amplification Post

**Used when:** Agency writes about a client launch they shipped. Different voice from the client's own post. Goal: social proof for the agency itself.
**Video role:** Client's launch video, reposted from agency handle.
**Length:** 1-2 tweets OR short thread.
**Structure beats:**
1. **Result-first hook** — "[Client] did [metric]. Here's the video we shipped."
2. **The process** — 1-2 lines ("3-week turnaround. Script + storyboard + live action + motion.")
3. **Video embedded**
4. **What came next** — second/third-order effects ("Week 2: 15 meetings booked.")
5. **Soft CTA** — "We take 3 launch slots per quarter. DM."

**Signature pattern (Renat / Represent style):**
> "We shipped [Client]'s launch video. Week 1: [first-order metric]. Month 3: [second-order metric]. Here's the 60 seconds."

**Signature pattern (Social Capital Inc style):**
> "[Client]'s launch thread hit [X] views. Here's what we changed in draft 4 that unlocked it."

**The upsell is implicit:** the result is the pitch. The agency name in the byline does the work.

**Real anchors:** Represent Studio amplification posts for Bilanc/Shadeform/Bardeen. Social Capital Inc client-launch posts.

## Format selection matrix

| Situation | Archetype |
|---|---|
| YC batch day, small audience | 1 (Single Tweet) |
| Strong personal story, new founder | 2 (Origin Thread) |
| Technically impressive build, AI-native | 3 (Teardown Thread) |
| Funding round just closed | 4 (Fundraise Thread) |
| Agency launching a free tool | 5 (Lead-Magnet) |
| Agency amplifying a client's win | 6 (Amplification) |

**If unsure between 1 and 2:** use 2 only if the origin story is genuinely unusual. Otherwise 1 converts better.
**If unsure between 3 and 4:** use 3 if the product itself is the flex, 4 if the validation (money, customers) is.
**Never blend 5 and 6 in one post** — pick one or you lose both.

---

## Cross-archetype rules

1. **Video placement.** Native video > link to YouTube. X and LinkedIn both penalize external links. If the video is >60s, upload native; if >2min, link to landing page inside reply tweet (not main tweet).
2. **Thread pacing.** Tweet 1 must stand alone (people read only it 60% of the time). Tweet 2 loses ~50% attention. Build the strongest content into tweets 1-3.
3. **The "reply tweet" trick.** Put the link/CTA as a reply to your own post, not in the main tweet. Keeps the main post algorithmically favored.
4. **Mentions cost reach.** Every @ in tweet 1 reduces impressions ~20%. Move mentions to the final tweet or reply.
5. **Hashtag cost.** Hashtags in the main post reduce reach on X. Acceptable on LinkedIn (1-3).
6. **Time-of-launch.** Tuesday/Wednesday 9-11am ET is the YC-calibrated sweet spot. Saturday is under-priced (Matt Epstein's data).
