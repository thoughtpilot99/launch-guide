# Video Integration — How the Post Wraps the Video

The launch video is the anchor asset. The post is the distribution wrapper. This file covers the *relationship* between post and video: where to embed, how the hook should match the video hook, and when the post should stand alone.

---

## The three video-post relationships

### A. Post WRAPS the video (most common)
The post is a frame around the video. Video auto-plays in feed. Text sets the stakes, gives context, and points to the next action.

**When:** Product launches, feature launches, demo-first AI products.
**Real anchors:** Bolt.new ("what do you want to build?"), Lovable 2.0 contest, Gamma Sites, Gamma 3.0, Crunched, Bilanc, Snowglobe.

**Placement rules:**
- **Native video** > YouTube link. X and LinkedIn suppress external links.
- **One unit of content.** Post + video = one unit. Don't split into reply.
- **Post length should match video length.** 60s video → single tweet / long tweet. 2-minute video → short thread (enough to sustain watch).
- **Match the hook.** If the video opens with a question, the post opens with the same question (or tighter version). Bilanc failed at this — strong video hook, weak post hook.

---

### B. Post STANDS ALONE (fundraise / milestone)
The post is the content. Video is optional (team photo, milestone graphic, maybe brand film).

**When:** Series A/B/E raises, ARR milestones, origin-story posts.
**Real anchors:** Lovable $200M, Lovable $330M, Gamma Series B, Deel $300M Series E, Deel $1B ARR reflection, Chasi YC announce.

**Placement rules:**
- Use a **team photo** or milestone image, not a demo video. Demo video here misdirects from the story.
- Keep paragraphs short. Long post + no video = requires readable pacing.
- **End with a tweet-sized thesis**, not a product CTA. "This is the age of the builder." / "All good things start small and shaky." / "Aim the chip in the right direction."

---

### C. Post TEASES a separate video (held reveal)
Tweet 1 sets up something impossible-sounding. Video is embedded in tweet 5-6, mid-thread, as the payoff.

**When:** Viral teardown / reveal threads. Technical showcase where the demo is the climax.
**Real anchors:** PJ Ace AI-ad threads, some Devin follow-ups, technical build threads.

**Placement rules:**
- Tweet 1 must promise something specific the video will deliver.
- Tweets 2-4 build the mechanism story (how we built it).
- Tweet 5 or 6 drops the video reveal.
- Last tweet gets the link.

**Risk:** High drop-off between tweets 1 and 5. Only use when story merits pacing.

---

## The Hook-Match Rule

**The post hook must match (or exceed) the video hook.**

Many launches ship with a strong video hook but a weak post hook. Example: Bilanc's video opens with "50 commits beats five commits, right?" (great hook). The post opens with "🚀 Launching Bilanc (YC W24): AI-Powered Engineering Productivity" (generic). The video carried the launch; the post left 2-3x on the table.

### Hook-match test
1. Read the video's opening 7 words.
2. Read the post's opening 7 words.
3. Does the post hook land as hard? If not, rewrite.

If the video says: "50 commits beats five commits, right?"
The post should say: "50 commits. Or 5. Which actually matters?"

If the video says: "I used to waste 6 hours on every Excel model. Now it takes 10 minutes."
The post should NOT say: "Today, we're proud to launch Crunched." It should say: "6 hours → 10 minutes. We just launched Crunched."

## Video length → post length matrix

| Video length | Default post | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 15-30s (feature teaser) | Single tweet | Video is self-explanatory |
| 30-60s (product overview) | Single tweet OR long tweet | Balanced |
| 60-120s (launch film) | Long tweet OR short thread | Post needs to sustain attention |
| 2-5min (brand/mission film) | Short thread (5-8) | Story requires pacing |
| 5min+ (documentary) | Long-form essay + teaser | Separate attention from launch moment |

---

## The "Watch the video" anti-CTA

**Do NOT write "Watch the video 👇" or "Full breakdown in the video."**

If the video is embedded, readers don't need the CTA — the thumbnail does the work. If the video is external, they won't click anyway.

**Instead:** make the post's last line intrigue-laden enough that the reader wants more context, and put a single link (NOT "watch here") in the reply tweet.

---

## The Video Hook / Post Hook lineup (for producers)

Before shipping, write these six lines side-by-side:

1. Video opening 7 words
2. Post opening 7 words
3. Video mid-point punchline (the quotable sentence inside the video)
4. Post's most-bookmarkable line
5. Video closing frame's ask (the CTA the video ends on)
6. Post's CTA

If any of these six feel significantly weaker than another, that's the weak link. Rewrite it before ship.

---

## When to SHOOT with the post in mind (production note)

If the agency has input at video stage, steer the video to produce:
- **A screenshot-worthy thumbnail** (for the Twitter card when the video unfolds)
- **A "reveal frame"** at the 5-10 second mark that will be the preview loop
- **A quotable sentence** inside the video that the post can lift verbatim
- **A last-frame URL** that matches the post's CTA

These four decisions save the post 90% of its writing time later.
