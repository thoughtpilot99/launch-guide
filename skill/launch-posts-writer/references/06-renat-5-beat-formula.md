# The Renat 5-Beat Formula (Canonical)

This is the **primary-source formula** for SaaS launch posts. Source: Renat Gabitov's own teaching video, built after analyzing 300+ launch posts. Every launch post the skill writes should map to these 5 beats.

**Source:** [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k50fTrT8q8o) | [corpus/18-renat-primary-formula.md](../corpus/18-renat-primary-formula.md)

---

## The 5 beats

### 1. Clear announcement (no fluff)
**Pattern:** `Introducing [Product], the [world's first / only / best] [Category].`
**Example:** "Introducing Icon, the world's first AI admaker."

**Rules:**
- Just the name + what it does in the easiest way to understand.
- No "we're excited to share" — kill throat-clearing.
- If your product is in a new category, say "world's first." If not, say "the best" or "the one that [specific differentiator]."

### 2. Problem statement (twist the knife)
**Pattern:** `[Plain statement of pain] + [adjective that activates memory].`
**Example:** "Making lots of ads is **extremely painful**."

**Rules:**
- Use adjectives that activate reader's memory: "brutally slow," "quietly expensive," "insanely manual."
- Don't list 5 problems. Pick ONE, sharpen it.
- The more the reader nods "yeah that's me," the stronger the beat.

### 3. Solution (one-sentence summary)
**Pattern:** `[Product] [verb] your [existing thing] into [better thing] + [quality guard].`
**Example:** "Icon remixes your existing footage into new ads matching your production quality."

**Rules:**
- One sentence.
- Include the "quality guard" phrase — "without [bad thing]" or "while still [good thing]."
- Verbs matter: "remixes," "indexes," "orchestrates," "simulates," "converts."

### 4. How it works (numbered list or arrow chain)
**Pattern:** `Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3 → Step 4.`
**Example:** "Icon tags your video library → generates scripts using a GPT → matches clips to scenes → you can edit fast."

**Rules:**
- 3-4 steps max.
- One line each. Terse.
- Arrows (→) or numbers. NOT bullets (bullets are LinkedIn-y).
- Renat's critique of Icon: "could have done better — it was a little too wordy."

### 5. Call to action (frictionless or engagement)
**Two branches:**

**A. Engagement tactic** (Renat's preference):
> "Comment [keyword] to get [free resource]."

**B. Direct link**:
> "[URL]" — but NEVER in the main post. In a reply tweet or comment.

**Rules:**
- Pick ONE branch, not both.
- Engagement tactic builds lead list and outperforms direct link for first-time founders.
- Direct link works for founders with large audiences.

## The HARD RULES (non-negotiable)

1. **NEVER put the link in the main post.** LinkedIn and X both suppress posts with external links. Put the link in a follow-up comment or reply tweet. Alternative: disguise with `.` in curly brackets (e.g., `lovable{.}dev`).

2. **Post from the FOUNDER's account.** Not the company account. Research: personal LinkedIn drives 2.75x impressions + 5x engagement (Refined Labs); employee posts get 14x more engagement (Valytics).

3. **Time it: Tuesday / Wednesday / Thursday at 9am PT.** Overlaps with Europe + East Coast + West Coast. Skip Mondays (planning calls), Fridays (mind elsewhere), weekends (people offline).

4. **Warm up the account 3-5 days before launch.** 15-20 min/day of commenting + liking on posts from people you know. They'll pay back the favor at launch.

5. **Engagement sprint: 15 min before + 2 hours after publish.** Respond thoughtfully to every comment. Comments beget comments beget algorithmic reach.

6. **The team reposts.** Every employee / cofounder / advisor reposts the main post from their own account. Company account is amplification ONLY.

## The analogy lever (force multiplier)

Every viral launch post in the corpus uses an analogy bridge. Renat's observation: *"When you introduce a completely new category of products, people need to compare it to something."*

**Template:** `[Product] is like [Familiar Thing A] + [Familiar Thing B], but for [new audience/use-case].`

**Examples from corpus:**
- **Icon:** "ChatGPT + CapCut, but for winning ads with AI in minutes."
- **Snowglobe:** (self-driving cars simulation applied to AI chatbots)
- **Lovable:** "Canva did for design what we're doing for code."
- **Cluely:** (positioned as cheating, reframed as tech-augmentation)
- **Nia:** "ChatGPT for code context"

**Rule:** pick ONE analogy. Pair with a number (scale, multiplier, or delta).

## The human component (trust-builder)

Renat's critique of launches that feel generic: *"Companies that come out of college dorm rooms trying to build the next AI for X."* The antidote = naming the founder's lived experience with the problem.

**Pattern examples:**
- **Snowglobe:** "Treya faced an issue of not having enough training data."
- **Crunched:** "Michael Sakowski (or deal-Mike as he was known at McK)..."
- **Deel:** "Shuo and I were in completely different worlds. She was in China, I was in Israel."
- **Cluely:** "I got kicked out of Columbia for building..."

**Rule:** one scene, one specific detail (nickname / city / time / number). Not a CV.

## Decision tree for the writer

**Q1: Does the product fit a "world's first" claim?**
- Yes → use "Introducing [Product], the world's first [Category]"
- No → use "the best [X] for [Y]" OR use a provocative hook (Cluely's "kicked out of Columbia")

**Q2: Is there a clean analogy?**
- Yes → include it in beat 3 or beat 1
- No → use a benchmark number vs a known brand (Nia: "27% better than Cursor")

**Q3: Does the founder have an unusual origin detail?**
- Yes → work it into beat 2 or beat 3
- No → lead with the product metric (Devin: "13.86% vs previous SOTA of 1.96%")

**Q4: What CTA?**
- Want leads → engagement tactic ("Comment X for Y")
- Want signups → direct link in reply
- Want brand thesis → close with a platform line ("This is the age of the builder.")

**Q5: What length?**
- Single tweet: product/benchmark speaks for itself, founder has audience
- Long tweet / long post: story is the asset
- Thread: >3 beats require pacing OR it's a teardown/reveal

## Pre-ship checklist (Renat-coded)

- [ ] Opens with product name + category in the first 10 words
- [ ] Problem statement uses an adjective that "twists the knife"
- [ ] One-sentence solution with quality guard
- [ ] 3-4 step "how it works" in arrows or numbers
- [ ] ONE CTA (engagement OR link, never both)
- [ ] Link is in reply/comment, NOT main post
- [ ] Founder's account, not company account
- [ ] Video is native, embedded in main post (if video launch)
- [ ] Publish Tue/Wed/Thu at 9am PT
- [ ] Team has been pinged to repost
- [ ] Account warmed up 3-5 days prior
- [ ] First 2 hours blocked for engagement sprint
