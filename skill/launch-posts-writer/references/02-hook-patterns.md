# Hook Patterns — The First 7 Words

The hook is the first sentence (often the first 7 words) of the launch post. On X you have ~280 chars to either earn the click on "Show more" or earn the swipe past. On LinkedIn you have ~210 chars before the "...see more" cutoff.

**Rule of thumb:** if the hook doesn't land, the video doesn't get watched. The post is the gate.

---

## The 12 Hook Archetypes (with raw examples and when to use each)

### 1. The Numbered-Proof Hook
**Pattern:** `I [analyzed/studied/watched/built] [big number] [things]. [Bold claim].`
**Why it works:** number = signals research = signals authority. Bold claim earns the read.
**Examples:**
- "I analyzed 250+ YC launch videos from 2025. 95% are doing it wrong."
- "We've shipped 130 launch films. The top 10% all do this one thing."
- "I watched 60 Series A announcement threads. Only 4 actually drove pipeline."

**Use for:** Teardown threads, framework posts, agency-amplification.
**Don't use for:** First-time founders with no portfolio (no number to claim).

---

### 2. The Distance / Constraint Hook
**Pattern:** Specific number + unlikely constraint.
**Why it works:** specificity = realness. Unlikely constraint = curiosity.
**Examples:**
- "I started a US company from 5,424km away. Today we're launching [X]." (Renat)
- "0 employees. $4M ARR. We just hired our first." (Pieter Levels-style)
- "27 days in San Francisco. We launched [X]."

**Use for:** Origin threads, fundraise threads where the journey is unusual.
**Don't use for:** Generic launches with no constraint backstory.

---

### 3. The "I Made This With AI" Shock Hook
**Pattern:** "I made this [video/site/product] with [N] prompts. [Existential frame]."
**Why it works:** Rides AI-discourse hype. Doubles as proof-of-work. Bait for QRT debate.
**Examples (Matt Epstein, 4 uses, top 27,053 engagement):**
- "We're sooo cooked. I made this whole launch video with 10 prompts. It's over."
- "I made this 60-second product film with 6 prompts. Creatives are dead."
- "Took me 90 minutes to make this with Sora. Agencies are toast."

**Use for:** AI-launch reactions, lead-magnet posts where the AI tool IS the thing.
**Don't use for:** Anything where craft is the point. This format eats your craft credibility.

**Cooldown:** 14-day minimum between uses on one account.

---

### 4. The Inversion Hook
**Pattern:** `The sure way to [fail/ruin/kill] [thing] is [counterintuitive Y].`
**Why it works:** Inversion is novel. Most launch advice is positive ("do X"). Negative framing breaks the scroll.
**Examples:**
- "The sure way to FAIL your startup launch is to ship a perfect video."
- "The fastest way to kill your launch is to over-prepare for it."
- "Want a dead launch? Polish your video for 6 weeks."

**Use for:** Contrarian thought-leadership, post-mortem threads.
**Don't use for:** Celebration moments (mood mismatch).

---

### 5. The Brutal-Truth Hook
**Pattern:** Flat indictment, present tense, no softener.
**Why it works:** Boldness earns the second sentence.
**Examples:**
- "Startups are terrible at activating users."
- "Most launch videos are wasted budget."
- "Your launch post will get 200 views and you don't know why."

**Use for:** Establishing authority before a teaching post. Pair with framework or proof.
**Don't use for:** Personal launches (sounds preachy).

---

### 6. The Side-by-Side Hook
**Pattern:** "Here are two [things] side by side. The difference is [Nx]."
**Why it works:** Visual promise + multiplier = save-worthy.
**Examples:**
- "Here are two of the same launch videos side by side and the difference is 7x."
- "Two thumbnails. Same product. One did 1.7M views. One did 12K."
- "Same script. Live-action vs motion. Watch the 3-second difference."

**Use for:** Teardowns, video-craft posts, A/B teaching.
**Don't use for:** Solo launches (you don't have the comparison).

---

### 7. The Personal-Journey Hook
**Pattern:** Naive past self + `Spoiler:` line.
**Why it works:** Vulnerability + reveal = magnetic.
**Examples:**
- "When I joined my first SaaS startup, I thought 'how hard can this be?' Spoiler: it was brutal."
- "3 years ago I was a consultant. Today I just shipped a $6M raise. Spoiler: the consulting helped more than I expected."
- "I quit a $400K job to build this. Spoiler: I'd do it again."

**Use for:** Origin threads, founder-journey posts.
**Don't use for:** Pure product launches.

---

### 8. The Cohort-Analysis Hook
**Pattern:** `I analyzed all [N] [cohort] from [year]. [Three / Five / N] traits separate the [winners / outliers].`
**Why it works:** Promised structure = read-to-the-end commitment.
**Examples:**
- "I analyzed all 250+ YC S25 launch videos. 3 traits separate the 1M+ from the rest."
- "I watched 100 Series A fundraise threads. 5 patterns repeat in the viral ones."

**Use for:** Framework / teaching posts (post-launch reflection, not the launch itself).
**Don't use for:** The actual launch tweet — too pedagogical.

---

### 9. The Unbelievable-Claim Hook (with proof in tweet 2)
**Pattern:** A number/claim that sounds fake. Tweet 2 proves it.
**Why it works:** Sets up a "no way → wait, really?" reading rhythm.
**Examples:**
- "We just hit 100K users in 28 days. (Receipts in tweet 2.)"
- "$6M raised the day after our launch video dropped."
- "Our launch video is 14 months old. It's still generating discovery calls today."

**Use for:** Result-led launch announcements. Compounding-angle posts.
**Don't use for:** Hooks where you can't actually deliver the proof.

---

### 10. The Result-First Hook (agency / amplification)
**Pattern:** `[Client] did [absurd metric]. Here's [the asset / how].`
**Why it works:** Buries the lede in service of the punch.
**Examples:**
- "Bilanc did 100K views from ONE LinkedIn post. Here's the 60-second video."
- "Crunched did 3M+ views on launch. Here's the video — and the 4 things we changed in draft 6."
- "Snowglobe's launch did 2M views. Here's what their landing page did differently."

**Use for:** Agency amplification posts, case study threads.
**Don't use for:** Founder's own launch posts (sounds boastful from the founder seat).

---

### 11. The Question Hook
**Pattern:** Pointed question that the post answers.
**Why it works:** Cognitive itch. Hard to scroll past an unanswered question you care about.
**Examples:**
- "Why do most YC launch videos die at 12K views?"
- "What separates a $6.2M-raise launch from a 50K-view launch?"
- "When does a launch video stop being an expense and start being an asset?"

**Use for:** Teaching posts, thought-leadership.
**Don't use for:** The launch tweet itself (no time for Q&A — declare).

---

### 12. The "Today We're Launching" Hook (the classic, hardest to make work)
**Pattern:** `Today, we're launching [Product] — [one-line value prop].`
**Why it works:** Direct, no games. Works ONLY when product/founder already has audience or product is genuinely novel.
**Examples:**
- "Today, we're launching Linear — a tool to track issues for software teams that move fast." (Linear v1)
- "Introducing Devin, the first AI software engineer." (Cognition)
- "Meet Lovable. Build apps by chatting." (Lovable)

**Use for:** Big-audience founders, well-funded launches with PR muscle, genuinely novel categories.
**Don't use for:** First-time founders without audience — you'll get 200 views.

---

## Hook anti-patterns (do not ship)

These look like hooks but are dead on arrival.

❌ **The Self-Reference Opener:** "As a founder, I..." / "I'm excited to share..." / "I've been working on something..."
❌ **The Vague Promise:** "Big news coming." / "Stay tuned." / "Something special dropping soon."
❌ **The Throat-Clear:** "After months of hard work..." / "Today is a special day for our team..."
❌ **The Hashtag Hook:** "#startuplaunch #saas #building" — only on LinkedIn, never in the hook position.
❌ **The Emoji-Only Hook:** "🚀🚀🚀" — does not bypass copy.
❌ **The Apology Hook:** "Sorry for the long absence..." — never apologize for time off in a launch post.
❌ **The Listicle Tease Without Structure:** "Here are 7 things..." — works only if tweet 2 actually has 7 things. Most fakers post 3.

---

## How to test a hook in <30 seconds

1. Read it OUT LOUD. If you stumble or feel embarrassed, it's wrong.
2. Strip the first 3 words. Does the rest still grab you? If yes, those 3 words were filler — cut them.
3. Could a competitor post the same hook? If yes, it's commodity — sharpen with a number, name, or constraint.
4. Does it earn tweet 2? If a reader scrolls past, did they miss something specific? If no, the hook didn't promise enough.
5. Does it match the video? If hook says "shocking" and video is sober, you'll get bounce. Tone-match.

## Hook scaffolding template (when stuck)

Fill in:
- **Number:** [a specific count, dollar, percent, or duration]
- **Subject:** [who/what did the thing]
- **Verb:** [shipped / built / raised / scraped / analyzed / made]
- **Outcome:** [the result, in concrete terms]
- **Bait:** [the line that makes them want tweet 2]

Then: collapse to 7-12 words. Cut adjectives. Cut "I think" / "we believe". Lead with the noun, not the verb.

Example:
> Number: 100K. Subject: One LinkedIn post. Verb: drove. Outcome: $1-2K MRR per customer. Bait: receipts in tweet 2.
> Drafted: "100K views on ONE LinkedIn post. ROI in tweet 2."
